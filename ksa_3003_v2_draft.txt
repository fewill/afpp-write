Q1 — Factual / JSQ

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Understanding that irrevocability makes faster payments
easier for fraudsters to abuse — making adequate onboarding a key fraud
prevention and legal risk mitigation tool — is foundational working
knowledge for a 2-year faster payments practitioner.

Why are customer onboarding programs considered especially important for
managing risk in faster payment systems?

  A. Because faster payment methods, especially those that are
     irrevocable, are easier for fraudsters to abuse, making adequate
     onboarding a key fraud prevention and legal risk mitigation tool
  B. Because faster payment systems require all participants to maintain
     a minimum customer base in order to sustain network liquidity and
     settlement coverage
  C. Because faster payment customers are exempt from standard credit
     review requirements and must be screened through separate
     onboarding-specific identity protocols

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 3 – Faster Payments Risk
Management, "Customer Onboarding" section, lines 8193–8210.

Q2 — Factual / JSQ

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Knowing that the USA PATRIOT Act is the governing law
requiring financial institutions to maintain a customer identification
program is foundational compliance knowledge for a 2-year faster
payments practitioner.

Under which federal law are financial institutions required to have a
customer identification program in place to verify account holder
identity and prevent fraudulent account openings?

  A. The Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act, which requires financial institutions
     to protect the privacy and security of customer financial
     information
  B. The USA PATRIOT Act, which requires financial institutions to have
     a customer identification program to verify account holder identity
     and prevent financial crimes
  C. The Electronic Fund Transfer Act, which requires financial
     institutions to establish procedures for verifying the identity of
     customers initiating electronic fund transfers

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 3 – Faster Payments Risk
Management, "Customer Onboarding" section, lines 8214–8218.

Q3 — Factual / SQ

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: The specific BSA record retention period of five years after
account closure — and its basis in 31 CFR 1010.430 — requires deeper
regulatory knowledge beyond the working-knowledge level expected of a
2-year practitioner.

According to federal BSA regulations, how long must a financial
institution retain records of the information used to verify a
customer's identity under its customer identification program?

  A. Three years after the account is opened, consistent with the
     institution's general record retention policy for account
     documentation
  B. Five years after the account is opened or until the account is
     closed, whichever is longer
  C. Five years after the account is closed

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 3 – Faster Payments Risk
Management, "Customer Onboarding" section, lines 8245–8249.
(31 CFR 1010.430)

Q4 — Procedural / JSQ

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: Knowing that a financial institution must file a Suspicious
Activity Report when its customer identification program fails to verify
a customer's identity is standard compliance knowledge for a 2-year
faster payments practitioner.

What action must a financial institution take when its customer
identification program process fails to verify a customer's identity?

  A. File a suspicious activity report with the Financial Crimes
     Enforcement Network
  B. Immediately close the account and return any funds deposited by
     the unverified customer to the originating institution
  C. Refer the account to the institution's credit department for an
     alternative identity verification review under the institution's
     standard credit approval process

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 3 – Faster Payments Risk
Management, Table 55 – Faster Payments Stakeholder Responsibilities in
the Customer Identification Program, lines 8265–8290.

Q5 — Procedural / JSQ

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: Knowing that Sender financial institutions must use
risk-based volume tracking to identify potential anomalous activity in
customer Request for Payment usage is a standard compliance process a
2-year faster payments practitioner should know.

When monitoring customer use of Request for Payment in the FedNow
Service, which procedure must a Sender financial institution follow?

  A. Suspend the customer's access to Request for Payment functionality
     pending completion of a quarterly risk review and submission of a
     certification to the Federal Reserve Bank
  B. Track the volume of Requests for Payment sent by customers using
     risk-based procedures to identify potential anomalous activity
  C. Require each customer to submit a written justification for each
     Request for Payment message sent in excess of the institution's
     internally defined threshold amount

Answer: B

Source: FedNow Service Operating Procedures (June 2025). Federal
Reserve Banks. Section on Request for Payment monitoring, lines
2051–2056.

Q6 — Procedural / SQ

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: The multi-step process — first informing the Sender financial
institution, then making a facts-based reasonable-diligence
determination before filing a warranty claim — requires expert knowledge
of FedNow Request for Payment Warranty procedures beyond the working-
knowledge level expected of a 2-year practitioner.

What must a Receiver financial institution do before filing a claim that
a Request for Payment was sent in breach of the RfP Warranty?

  A. Submit a formal dispute to the Federal Reserve Bank within the
     applicable timeframe and provide documentation that the end
     customer declined to initiate a credit transfer in response to the
     Request for Payment
  B. Obtain a signed statement from the end customer confirming that
     the Request for Payment was unsolicited and that no underlying
     transaction existed to support the request for funds
  C. Inform the Sender financial institution of the allegedly breaching
     Request for Payment and then make a determination, based on facts
     collected through reasonable diligence, that the RfP Warranty was
     breached

Answer: C

Source: FedNow Service Operating Procedures (June 2025). Federal
Reserve Banks. Section on Request for Payment Warranty claims, lines
2076–2081.

Q7 — Conditional / JSQ

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The clear, single-rule condition — that a business customer's
Request for Payment must relate to a current sale, transaction, or
previously agreed amount — is a straightforward warranty requirement a
2-year practitioner handling Request for Payment onboarding should be
able to apply.

Under what condition does a business customer's use of Request for
Payment fail to satisfy the legitimate purpose requirement of the RfP
Warranty?

  A. When the business customer sends a Request for Payment for an
     amount that is NOT related to a current sale, transaction, or a
     sum previously agreed to be paid by the receiving customer
  B. When the business customer sends a Request for Payment for an
     amount that exceeds the threshold established by the Sender
     financial institution's internally configured transaction
     monitoring system
  C. When the business customer sends a Request for Payment to a
     receiving customer who has NOT completed the zero-dollar Request
     for Payment readiness assessment recommended by the FedNow Service

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 3 – Faster Payments Risk
Management, "Customer Onboarding" section, lines 8292–8306. Also:
FedNow Service Operating Procedures (June 2025). Federal Reserve Banks.
Section on Request for Payment Warranty, lines 2037–2047.

Q8 — Conditional / SQ

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The specific conditions under which a financial institution
may rely on another institution's customer identification program
procedures — requiring that the other institution be federally regulated
and have adequate AML procedures — involves regulatory nuance about
inter-institution reliance that requires 5+ years of compliance
expertise to apply reliably.

Under what condition may a financial institution rely on another
financial institution's customer identification program procedures for
a shared customer?

  A. When both financial institutions have executed a formal customer
     identification program sharing agreement and submitted the
     agreement to their primary federal regulator for approval prior to
     account opening
  B. When the other financial institution is regulated by a federal
     financial regulator and has adequate anti-money laundering
     procedures in place
  C. When the customer has provided written consent authorizing the
     sharing of identity verification information between the two
     financial institutions prior to account opening

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 3 – Faster Payments Risk
Management, Table 55 – Faster Payments Stakeholder Responsibilities in
the Customer Identification Program, lines 8265–8290.

Q9 — Conditional / SQ

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: Applying the individual-sender prong of the RfP Warranty —
that the receiver must be known to the consumer sender and reasonably
expected to receive the request — requires synthesis of the two-prong
legitimate-purpose test (business sender vs. consumer sender) and the
ability to distinguish the consumer standard from the business standard,
a nuanced regulatory analysis characteristic of a 5-year practitioner.

Under what condition does a consumer sender's use of Request for Payment
comply with the RfP Warranty's legitimate purpose requirement?

  A. When the consumer sender confirms in writing to the Sender
     financial institution that the amount requested reflects a
     mutually agreed-upon obligation and attaches documentation of the
     underlying transaction
  B. When the consumer sender limits the Request for Payment to amounts
     that fall below the maximum dollar threshold established for
     consumer-initiated faster payments under applicable Federal Reserve
     guidelines
  C. When the Request for Payment is sent to a receiver who is known to
     the consumer sender and who is reasonably expected to receive the
     request from that sender

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 3 – Faster Payments Risk
Management, "Customer Onboarding" section, lines 8292–8306. Also:
FedNow Service Operating Procedures (June 2025). Federal Reserve Banks.
Section on Request for Payment Warranty, lines 2037–2047.
