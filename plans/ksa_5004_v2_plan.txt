Plan — KSA 5004 (v2)
The Role of Client/Customer Agreements and Disclosures with Respect to
Faster Payments Products and Services
=========================================================================

KSA: 5000 – Governance Framework
  5004 – The role of client/customer agreements and disclosures with
         respect to faster payments products and services
    – Identify the required agreements for each payment rail
    – Identify the required disclosures for each payment rail
    – Describe the roles of agreements among faster payments stakeholders
    – Describe the roles of disclosures among faster payments
      stakeholders
    – Compare warranties and indemnities by rail and rule set

Question distribution: Factual (2 JSQ + 1 SQ) | Procedural (2 JSQ + 1 SQ)
                       Conditional (1 JSQ + 2 SQ)
Target answer distribution: A(3), B(3), C(3)

Note: v1 questions covered Same Day ACH origination agreement purpose;
Visa Direct Terms and Conditions disclosure before service use;
Mastercard Send USD 250,000 liability cap; FedNow RfP warranty breach
camt.056/WNTB/95-day process; RTP TCH liable only for gross negligence
or intentional misconduct; Same Day ACH gateway operator assumes ODFI
warranties; FedNow OC8 indemnification trigger; Visa Direct conditional
liability (directly attributable to Visa); and FedNow special/
consequential damages prohibition. All nine v2 questions draw on
different passages and topics.

SOURCE MATERIAL SUMMARY
=========================================================================

Sub-competency 1 — Required agreements: RTP
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
AFPP Handbook, lines 10087–10094:
  RTP participants must execute a Participant Agreement and Indemnity,
  which ensures participants agree to network governance terms and
  conditions, requires adherence to RTP rules and security protocols,
  and includes the participant's indemnification of The Clearing House
  for liabilities arising from network access.

RTP Participation Rules (July 21, 2025), Section IV.A.1, page 5:
  "A prospective Participant must execute and deliver, directly or
  through its Third-Party Service Provider, the Participant Agreement
  and Indemnity, and any other agreement that may be required by TCH."

RTP Participation Rules, Section IV.B.1, page 5:
  A Participant Agreement and Indemnity entered with a Third-Party
  Service Provider must include provisions by which the Participant
  agrees: (a) to the RTP Participant Terms and Conditions; (b) to
  comply with the RTP Participation Rules and RTP Operating Rules;
  (c) that the Third-Party Service Provider's agreement cannot amend
  or modify the Participant Agreement and Indemnity; and (d) that TCH
  and other Participants are intended third-party beneficiaries of the
  Participant Agreement and Indemnity.

Sub-competency 1 — Required agreements: FedNow (OC8)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Operating Circular 8 (April 1, 2026), Section 8.2, page 11:
  "Before issuing a payment order or receiving a payment order from a
  Reserve Bank, a FedNow Participant must execute an agreement in the
  form shown in Appendix A to this operating circular [the FedNow
  Service Security Procedure Agreement]."

Operating Circular 8, Sections 15.1–15.2, page 20:
  A FedNow Participant may authorize a Service Provider by executing
  the agreement in Appendix B to OC8. By executing that agreement, the
  Participant: (i) authorizes the Service Provider to act on its behalf
  without limitation; (ii) designates the Service Provider as its agent
  for accessing Reserve Bank systems; and (iii) authorizes the Service
  Provider to select which security procedures to use.

Operating Circular 8, Section 15.13, page 22:
  Designation of a Service Provider does not diminish any obligation of
  the FedNow Participant under OC8 or any separate operating circular.
  The FedNow Participant retains full responsibility for management of
  its Master Account and for ensuring the Service Provider complies
  with the Participant's obligations.

Sub-competency 2 — Required disclosures: RTP
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
AFPP Handbook, lines 10155–10164:
  TCH must disclose pricing, exceptions handling, policies, practices,
  and standards to participants. Participants must disclose pricing,
  standards, and rules for RTP payments, and exceptions processing
  handling, to customers using the service. Funding participants must
  disclose their relationships with non-funding participants to TCH.
  TCH provides reconciliation reports to participants at day's end;
  funding participants furnish reconciliation reports to non-funding
  participants detailing activities.

Sub-competency 2 — Required disclosures: Mastercard Send
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
AFPP Handbook, lines 10186–10191:
  Participating financial institutions must: (1) receive necessary
  standards information on service providers, including required privacy
  policies, fees/pricing, and applicable standards before becoming
  customers; (2) regularly disclose customer transaction activity
  reports to Mastercard; and (3) disclose all fees and appropriate
  information to customers using Mastercard Send in accordance with
  applicable laws.

Sub-competency 3/5 — Role of agreements: RTP TFSP agreement content
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
RTP Participation Rules, Section IV.B.1, page 5 (see above):
  Key provision: TCH and other Participants are intended third-party
  beneficiaries of any agreement between a Participant and its Third-
  Party Service Provider. This means the TFSP agreement cannot be used
  to modify or reduce the Participant's obligations to TCH or to other
  RTP participants.

Sub-competency 5 — Warranties/indemnities: Nacha Operating Rules
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
AFPP Handbook, lines 10211–10214:
  ODFI indemnification: ODFIs indemnify RDFIs and ACH Operators for
  claims, losses, liabilities, and expenses (including legal fees)
  stemming from breaches of warranties made by the ODFI under the
  Nacha Operating Rules, or from debiting/crediting entries per entry
  terms (including return of items from insufficient funds caused by a
  debit entry).

  RDFI indemnification: RDFIs indemnify ODFIs and ACH Operators
  against claims, losses, liabilities, and expenses resulting from any
  breach of warranty under the general rule compliance provision
  (Section 1.2) of the Nacha Operating Rules.

Sub-competency 5 — Warranties/indemnities: OC8 Service Provider
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Operating Circular 8, Section 15.15, page 22:
  The FedNow Participant and the Service Provider shall each indemnify
  and hold the Reserve Bank harmless against any claim, loss, cost, or
  expense — including attorneys' fees and litigation expenses —
  resulting from: (i) the designation or use of the Service Provider;
  (ii) the acts or omissions of the Participant or the Service Provider
  (or any of their agents), other than a loss arising solely from the
  Reserve Bank's failure to exercise ordinary care or to act in good
  faith; or (iii) the Participant's failure to authorize the Service
  Provider through a properly executed Appendix B agreement.

Sub-competency 5 — Warranties/indemnities: FedNow third-party products
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
AFPP Handbook, lines 10245–10249:
  Reserve Banks disclaim liability for any loss or damage arising from
  a financial institution's or Service Provider's use of third-party
  products or services NOT supplied, owned, or operated by the Reserve
  Banks. This disclaimer extends to any warranty — express or implied
  — concerning accuracy, timeliness, completeness, merchantability,
  fitness for a particular purpose, title, quality, or non-infringement
  of such third-party products or services.

Q1 — Factual / JSQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: Required agreements — RTP
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Knowing that a prospective RTP participant must execute a
Participant Agreement and Indemnity that requires adherence to network
governance, security protocols, and includes indemnification of The
Clearing House is foundational knowledge for a 2-year faster payments
practitioner.

Stem:
  What must a prospective RTP participant execute as the primary
  agreement before joining the RTP network?

Responses:
  A. The RTP Participant Agreement and Indemnity, which requires
     adherence to network governance terms, security protocols, and
     transaction processing standards, and includes the participant's
     indemnification of The Clearing House for liabilities arising from
     network access
  B. The FedNow Service Security Procedure Agreement, which must be
     executed before the participant can send or receive payment orders
     through the Federal Reserve Banks
  C. The Nacha Operating Rules origination agreement, which binds the
     participant to the Nacha Operating Rules and authorizes the
     Originating Depository Financial Institution to receive
     transactions on the participant's behalf

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Required Agreements — RTP" section, lines 10087–10094. Also: RTP
Participation Rules (July 21, 2025). The Clearing House. Section
IV.A.1, page 5.

Q2 — Factual / JSQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: Required disclosures — RTP
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Knowing that The Clearing House must disclose pricing,
exceptions handling, policies, practices, and standards to RTP
participants — and provide end-of-day reconciliation reports — is
working knowledge for a 2-year practitioner managing RTP compliance.

Stem:
  According to the AFPP Handbook, which of the following must The
  Clearing House disclose to RTP participants?

Responses:
  A. The identity of all other participants accessing the RTP network
     during each settlement period, along with their individual
     transaction volumes and prefunded balance positions
  B. Pricing, exceptions handling, policies, practices, and standards
     related to RTP payments, as well as end-of-day reconciliation
     reports detailing settlement activities
  C. The settlement account balance of each funding participant as of
     the close of each business day, along with any overdraft exposure
     incurred during processing

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Required Disclosures — RTP" section, lines 10155–10164.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q3 — Factual / SQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: Warranties and indemnities — Nacha Operating Rules RDFI
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: The specific scope of the RDFI's indemnification obligation
— arising from breaches of the general rule compliance warranty under
Section 1.2 of the Nacha Operating Rules — requires detailed knowledge
of the indemnity provisions beyond 2-year working knowledge.

Stem:
  Under the Nacha Operating Rules, for what must a Receiving Depository
  Financial Institution indemnify an Originating Depository Financial
  Institution and the ACH Operator?

Responses:
  A. Losses resulting from fraudulent debit entries that the Receiving
     Depository Financial Institution posted to consumer accounts
     without first confirming authorization with the Originating
     Depository Financial Institution
  B. Costs and expenses arising from the Receiving Depository Financial
     Institution's failure to make available funds received within the
     applicable next-day availability timeframe
  C. Claims, losses, liabilities, and expenses resulting from any
     breach of warranty by the Receiving Depository Financial
     Institution under the general rule compliance provision of the
     Nacha Operating Rules

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Warranties and Indemnities — Same Day ACH" section, lines 10213–10214.

Q4 — Procedural / JSQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: Required disclosures — Mastercard Send
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: Knowing that Mastercard Send requires participating financial
institutions to disclose all fees and appropriate information to
customers in accordance with applicable laws is a standard disclosure
obligation a 2-year practitioner should know.

Stem:
  According to the AFPP Handbook, what fee disclosure obligation do
  financial institutions have toward customers using the Mastercard
  Send service?

Responses:
  A. Financial institutions must disclose all fees and appropriate
     information to customers using the Mastercard Send service in
     accordance with applicable laws and regulations
  B. Financial institutions must submit fee schedules to Mastercard for
     pre-approval before disclosing them to customers, with updates
     required within 30 days of any fee change
  C. Financial institutions must provide customers with a standardized
     comparison of Mastercard Send fees relative to other available
     faster payment services at the time of account opening

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Required Disclosures — Mastercard Send" section, lines 10186–10191.

Q6 — Procedural / SQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: Role of agreements — RTP TFSP third-party beneficiary
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: The requirement that a Participant's agreement with its
Third-Party Service Provider designate TCH and other Participants as
intended third-party beneficiaries — preventing the TFSP agreement from
modifying the Participant Agreement — is a specific RTP Participation
Rules provision that requires 5+ years of expertise to identify and
apply correctly.

Stem:
  According to the RTP Participation Rules, which provision must be
  included in an agreement between an RTP participant and its Third-
  Party Service Provider?

Responses:
  A. A requirement that the Third-Party Service Provider establish a
     separate prefunded balance account at The Clearing House
     sufficient to cover the participant's aggregate daily transaction
     volume
  B. A requirement that the Third-Party Service Provider obtain The
     Clearing House's prior written approval before initiating payment
     messages on behalf of the participant
  C. A provision designating The Clearing House and other Participants
     as intended third-party beneficiaries of the agreement between
     the Participant and the Third-Party Service Provider

Answer: C

Source: RTP Participation Rules (July 21, 2025). The Clearing House.
Section IV.B.1, page 5.

Q7 — Conditional / JSQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: Warranties and indemnities — Same Day ACH ODFI
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The clear, single-rule condition — that an ODFI must
indemnify the RDFI when it breaches a Nacha Operating Rules warranty
or when its debit entry causes a return due to insufficient funds —
is a foundational ACH indemnity principle a 2-year practitioner should
be able to apply.

Stem:
  Under the Nacha Operating Rules, when must an Originating Depository
  Financial Institution indemnify a Receiving Depository Financial
  Institution?

Responses:
  A. When the Originating Depository Financial Institution breaches a
     warranty made under the Nacha Operating Rules or debits an entry
     that results in the return of items from the receiving account
     due to insufficient funds
  B. When the Originating Depository Financial Institution fails to
     process a reversing entry within the required timeframe and the
     Receiving Depository Financial Institution incurs costs in
     notifying the originator that the correction was not applied
  C. When the Originating Depository Financial Institution's
     origination agreement with the originator is found to be
     incomplete or unenforceable and the Receiving Depository
     Financial Institution incurs losses in posting the original entry

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Warranties and Indemnities — Same Day ACH" section, lines 10211–10212.

Q8 — Conditional / SQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: Warranties and indemnities — OC8 Service Provider
               indemnification trigger
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The specific indemnification trigger in OC8 Section 15.15
— covering acts or omissions of the Participant or Service Provider
except when the loss arises solely from the Reserve Bank's failure to
exercise ordinary care — requires synthesis of OC8's multi-condition
indemnity framework characteristic of a 5-year practitioner.

Stem:
  Under Operating Circular 8, under what condition must a FedNow
  participant indemnify and hold the Federal Reserve Bank harmless in
  connection with the participant's use of a Service Provider?

Responses:
  A. When the Service Provider fails to meet the minimum security
     standards outlined in the FedNow Service Operating Procedures
     and the participant had prior written notice of the deficiency
     before continuing to use the Service Provider
  B. When the acts or omissions of the participant or the Service
     Provider result in a claim, loss, cost, or expense to the Reserve
     Bank, except when the loss arises solely from the Reserve Bank's
     failure to exercise ordinary care or to act in good faith
  C. When the participant exceeds its designated monthly transaction
     volume through the Service Provider and the resulting processing
     delays cause settlement failures during a Federal Reserve
     settlement window

Answer: B

Source: Operating Circular 8 (April 1, 2026). Federal Reserve Banks.
FedNow Service. Section 15.15, page 22.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q9 — Conditional / SQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: Warranties and indemnities — FedNow third-party product
               disclaimer
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The specific condition under which the Federal Reserve Banks
disclaim all liability for third-party products — when those products
are NOT supplied, owned, or operated by the Reserve Banks — and the
breadth of the disclaimer (covering all express and implied warranties)
requires synthesis of FedNow's multi-layered liability framework
characteristic of a 5-year practitioner.

Stem:
  Under what condition do the Federal Reserve Banks disclaim liability
  for a FedNow participant's loss or damage in connection with a third-
  party product or service?

Responses:
  A. When the loss or damage results from the participant's failure to
     notify the Reserve Banks within 5 business days that the third-
     party product or service did not conform to the participant's
     specifications
  B. When the participant used the third-party product or service for
     purposes outside the scope of its FedNow participation, including
     internal payment processing or non-FedNow transaction activity
  C. When the third-party product or service was NOT supplied, owned,
     or operated by the Reserve Banks, regardless of any warranty
     concerning accuracy, completeness, merchantability, or fitness
     for a particular purpose

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Warranties and Indemnities — FedNow" section, lines 10245–10249.
