Q1 — Factual / JSQ

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Knowing that a FedNow receive-only participant can send RfPs
despite being unable to initiate credit transfers is foundational knowledge
for a 2-year FedNow practitioner.

Which action can a FedNow receive-only participant perform even though it
cannot initiate customer credit transfers?

  A. Send a Request for Payment to a financial institution that can initiate
     a credit transfer in response
  B. Receive a Request for Payment from a financial institution that needs
     the receive-only participant to fund a payment
  C. Initiate a Liquidity Management Transfer to prefund its settlement
     account at the Federal Reserve

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 1 – Fundamentals of Faster
Payments, "FedNow participant types" section, lines 3031–3034.

Q2 — Factual / JSQ

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Knowing that a Visa Direct Recipient Issuer must post an OCT to
the Payment Credential within 2 business days of receipt — or dispute it to
the Originating Acquirer — is a foundational operational requirement a
2-year Visa Direct practitioner should recognize.

Which action must a Visa Direct Recipient Issuer take within 2 business
days of receiving an Original Credit Transaction?

  A. Submit a Request for Information to the Originating Acquirer to verify
     the sender's identity and confirm the transaction was properly
     authorized before posting the funds
  B. Post the Original Credit Transaction to the Payment Credential or
     initiate a dispute to the Originating Acquirer
  C. Make funds immediately available to the cardholder if the Recipient
     Issuer participates in the Visa Direct Fast Funds program

Answer: B

Source: Visa Core Rules and Visa Product and Service Rules. Section
8.4.2.2 – OCT Recipient Issuer Requirements, page 594.

Q3 — Factual / SQ

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Knowing that a FedNow Settlement-Only participant is prohibited
from sending or receiving both pacs.009 (FI credit transfers) and pacs.008
(customer credit transfers) — while still being able to exchange system
messages — requires deep knowledge of FedNow's participation type matrix
beyond 2-year working knowledge.

Which message types are prohibited for a FedNow Settlement-Only
participant?

  A. pacs.009 (Financial Institution Credit Transfer) and pacs.002 (Payment
     Status Report)
  B. admi.002 (Message Reject) and camt.060 (Account Reporting Request)
  C. pacs.009 (Financial Institution Credit Transfer) and pacs.008 (Customer
     Credit Transfer)

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 1 – Fundamentals of Faster
Payments, "Liquidity management" Page 49.

Q4 — Procedural / JSQ

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: Knowing that a FedNow Credit Transfer Receive-Only participant
is prohibited from sending pacs.008 is a standard practitioner-level message
type rule encountered during onboarding and compliance reviews.

Which message type is a FedNow Credit Transfer Receive-Only participant
prohibited from sending?

  A. pacs.008 (Customer Credit Transfer), which the participant can only
     receive, not originate
  B. pacs.002 (Payment Status Report), which can only be sent by
     Send-and-Receive participants
  C. pacs.004 (Payment Return), which receive-only participants are NOT
     permitted to initiate under any condition

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 1 – Fundamentals of Faster
Payments, Table 22 – Message Types by Participant – FedNow, lines
3564–3617.

Q5 — Procedural / JSQ

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: Knowing that RTP Receiving-Only participants must both send AND
receive pacs.002 — while being prohibited from sending pacs.008 — is a
working-level message type rule for RTP operations practitioners.

Which message type must an RTP Receiving-Only participant both send and
receive?

  A. pacs.008 (Customer Credit Transfer), which all RTP participants must
     be able to send and receive to maintain network reachability
  B. pacs.002 (Payment Status Report), which Receiving-Only participants
     must send to acknowledge receipt and receive to confirm payment status
  C. pain.013 (Request for Payment), which is required for all participant
     types that do NOT initiate credit transfers

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 1 – Fundamentals of Faster
Payments, Table 23 – Example of Message Types by Participant – RTP,
lines 3689–3725.

Q6 — Procedural / SQ

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: The specific rule that a FedNow Credit Transfer Receive-Only
participant may send pain.013 (RfP) but is prohibited from receiving
pain.013 — because receiving an RfP requires the ability to respond with a
credit transfer — requires deep knowledge of the FedNow message permission
matrix beyond a 2-year practitioner's working knowledge.

Which describes a FedNow Credit Transfer Receive-Only participant's
permissions for the pain.013 (Request for Payment) message?

  A. The participant is prohibited from both sending and receiving pain.013
     because it cannot initiate or respond to credit transfer requests
  B. The participant must receive pain.013 messages as a mandatory
     requirement but is prohibited from sending them
  C. The participant may optionally send pain.013 messages but is prohibited
     from receiving them

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 1 – Fundamentals of Faster
Payments, Table 22 – Message Types by Participant – FedNow, lines
3649–3658. Also: lines 3542–3549.

Q7 — Conditional / JSQ

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The rule that a financial institution choosing to originate ACH
entries must also agree to act as an RDFI is a clear, single-rule condition
under the Nacha Operating Rules that a 2-year ACH practitioner should know.

Under what condition must a financial institution that originates ACH
entries also agree to act as a Receiving Depository Financial Institution
in the Same Day ACH network?

  A. Whenever the financial institution chooses to originate ACH entries,
     regardless of entry type or origination volume
  B. When the financial institution's total origination volume exceeds the
     threshold established by the ACH Operator for the current processing
     window
  C. When the financial institution uses a third-party service provider to
     forward ACH entries to the ACH Operator on its behalf

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 1 – Fundamentals of Faster
Payments, "Same Day ACH participant types" section, lines 3080–3081.

Q8 — Conditional / SQ

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The rule that an RTP Participant is fully responsible to The
Clearing House and other Participants for all acts and omissions of its
Third-Party Service Provider — not just for out-of-scope actions — requires
knowledge of the specific TCH RTP Participation Rules liability framework
characteristic of a 5-year practitioner.

Under what condition is an RTP Participant liable to The Clearing House and
other Participants for actions taken by its Third-Party Service Provider in
connection with the RTP System?

  A. Only when the Third-Party Service Provider takes actions outside the
     scope of the services described in the Participant's agreement with
     The Clearing House
  B. At all times, for any act or omission of the Third-Party Service
     Provider with respect to the RTP System and the Participant's
     obligations under the RTP Participation Rules and RTP Operating Rules
  C. Only when The Clearing House has formally determined that the
     Third-Party Service Provider is no longer eligible to provide services
     to RTP Participants

Answer: B

Source: RTP Participation Rules (The Clearing House, July 21, 2025).
Section III – Third-Party Service Providers, Part D.1 – Participant
Liability for Third-Party Service Providers, page 4.

Q9 — Conditional / SQ

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The specific FedNow obligation that triggers a Receive-Only
participant's sign-off requirement — planned downtime that affects its
ability to respond to pacs.008 and pacs.004 within the reserved response
time — requires synthesis of participation type obligations and message
type requirements in the FedNow Operating Procedures.

Under what condition must a FedNow Credit Transfer Receive-Only participant
sign off from the FedNow Service using a participant broadcast message
(admi.004)?

  A. When the participant transitions from a Receive-Only participation type
     to a Send and Receive participation type and requires a new connection
     profile
  B. When the participant's Federal Reserve Master Account balance falls
     below the minimum threshold established by the Federal Reserve Bank
     for intraday overdraft capacity
  C. When planned downtime affects the participant's ability to respond to
     pacs.008 and pacs.004 messages within the reserved response time or to
     immediately make funds available to customers

Answer: C

Source: Federal Reserve Banks. FedNow Service Operating Procedures
(June 24, 2025), "Planned Downtime" section, lines 426–428.
