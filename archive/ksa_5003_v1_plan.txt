SECTION A: Definition of an Error on a Faster Payment
  Source: AFPP Handbook, lines 9873–9881
  Section heading: "Error on a faster payment" (Chapter 5 – Governance Framework)

  Key passage (lines 9873–9881):
  "An error refers to an unintended deviation or mistake that occurs in the
  processing of a faster payment. There are different types of errors depending
  on which stage of the payment the error happens including authentication,
  processing, authorization, settlement, and reconciliation errors. For instance,
  TCH defines payment errors related to RTP payments as when the payment amount
  is erroneous, a payment is sent to an unintended Receiving Participant or
  Receiver, or an RTP payment is sent twice (duplicate payment). Error resolution
  includes the process of investigating whether or not an erroneous, unauthorized,
  or fraudulent faster payment transaction has been made and the corrective
  measures taken by users and financial institutions. Error resolution will differ
  based on the type of error as well as the rail involved."

  Irrevocability context (lines 5969–5973):
  "Some types of faster payments are inherently irrevocable due to the speed of
  their payments processing. Therefore, the only way funds can be recovered in
  case of a mistake is through a payment return. However, for a payment to be
  returned the recipient of the original transaction must consent to the funds to
  be sent back to the originator."

SECTION B: Applicability of Regulation E vs. UCC Article 4A
  Source: AFPP Handbook, lines 9882–9902; FFIEC booklet, lines 571–594
  Section heading: "Applicability of error resolution protections under
  Regulation E and UCC4A"

  Regulation E scope (AFPP Handbook, lines 9883–9897):
  "Regulation E governs electronic fund transfers initiated to debit or credit a
  consumer account... Regulation E provides specific rights and protections for
  consumers when errors occur in electronic payments, including specifications as
  to the timeframe for reporting errors and investigation procedures for financial
  institutions. Regulation E also defines instances when consumers are entitled to
  provisional credit for disputed amounts during investigations and ensures the
  timely resolution of errors, thus safeguarding consumers' interests and protects
  them from errors, unauthorized transactions, and fraudulent activities."

  UCC Article 4A scope (AFPP Handbook, lines 9898–9902):
  "Article 4A of the UCC provides a framework for the rights and obligations of
  banks and businesses involved in funds transfers. It focuses on defining the
  responsibilities of entities involved in commercial transactions such as giving
  notice to counterparties of erroneous or unauthorized transfers and
  investigating and resolving errors for recipient entities when receiving notice.
  Article 4A also specifies liability allocation, enforcement mechanisms, and
  remedies."

  FFIEC booklet distinction (lines 571–594):
  - Consumer ACH: governed by ACH rules and Regulation E
  - Consumer unauthorized checks: governed by UCC and Regulation CC
  - Unlike Regulation E, the UCC does not contain specific re-credit procedures
    that a financial institution must follow.

SECTION C: Payer Liability for a Misdirected Payment
  Source: AFPP Handbook, lines 9903–9922
  Section heading: "Payer liability for a misdirected payment"

  Under Regulation E (lines 9916–9919):
  "Payers must give an oral and, upon request from its FI, a written notice of
  the error made within 60 days of receiving a periodic statement or other
  documentation first reflecting the alleged error from its FI. The notice must
  include the customer's name and account number, an explanation for why the error
  exists as well as the error's type, date, and amount."

  Under UCC Article 4A (lines 9920–9922):
  "Payers must disclose an error related to misdirected payments and notify their
  bank within 90 days of receiving payment confirmation from their bank. If the
  payer fails to do so, he or she is liable for the amount sent and not more."

SECTION D: FI Error Resolution Responsibilities — Regulation E
  Source: AFPP Handbook, lines 9943–9955 (Table 62)
  Section heading: "Table 62: Liabilities and Error Resolution Responsibilities
  of Financial Institutions by Payment Rail"

  Key requirements (lines 9943–9955):
  - Require written confirmation of error from consumer no later than 10 days
    after receiving oral notice.
  - Promptly investigate the allegation.
  - Complete investigation within 10 business days.
  - Report findings within 3 business days of completing the investigation.
  - Correct the error within one business day after determining error was made.
  - Extension option: FI may extend investigation to 45 calendar days if it
    provides provisional funds to the consumer within the original 10 business
    days, advises the consumer within 2 business days of the provisional credit,
    and gives full use of the funds during the extended investigation.
  - If no error found: provide written explanation including documents used to
    make the determination.

SECTION E: FI Error Resolution Responsibilities — UCC Article 4A
  Source: AFPP Handbook, lines 9956–9962

  Receiving FI obligations (lines 9956–9962):
  "Receiving financial institutions are required to correct errors they made when
  executing payment orders, whether the error was related to the amount or the
  identity of the beneficiary. This includes:
  - Collecting an additional amount from the sender if the amount debited to the
    beneficiary is lower than the original payment order.
  - Recovering and returning the excess funds if the amount collected is larger
    than the original payment order.
  - Recovering and returning the full amount of a payment order that was debited
    to a beneficiary different to the one specified in the payment order.
  Note: these obligations apply only when UCC Article 4A governs the transaction,
  which typically excludes consumer accounts unless otherwise agreed."

SECTION F: FedNow Return Request Timeframes
  Source: FedNow Operating Procedures, lines 4126–4131, 4219–4220

  Return request window (lines 4126–4131):
  "A Customer Credit Transfer (pacs.008) can be returned either when a FedNow
  Participant decides to honor a return request (camt.056) message or when the
  FedNow Participant cannot apply the funds it received... If needed, a return
  request (camt.056) should be sent within 60 calendar days from when the
  Customer Credit Transfer (pacs.008) settled."

  Note: camt.056 messages used to report fraud or an RFP warranty breach are
  not subject to the 60-day guideline. (FedNow Operating Procedures, lines
  4191–4193)

SECTION G: FedNow Accept Without Posting (ACWP) and Error Investigation
  Source: FedNow Operating Procedures, lines 3279–3291

  ACWP framework (lines 3279–3291):
  "Accept Without Posting (ACWP) is one of the three available responses a
  Receiver FI can use in its initial response to an instant payment (pacs.008 or
  pacs.004). When ACWP is used in response to an instant payment, the FedNow
  Service settles the transaction with finality... The Federal Reserve Banks
  require a Participant using the ACWP response to reject the transaction (and
  return funds) or make funds available to the intended recipient by midnight
  Eastern Time of the next standard business day (Monday through Friday, except
  holidays), unless the Participant continues to be concerned that the recipient
  is not entitled to receive the payment and taking either action would be
  impermissible under applicable law. In this case, the Federal Reserve Banks
  require the Participant to provide a status of pending (PDNG) response to the
  FedNow Service sender by the ACWP availability deadline."

----- FACTUAL QUESTIONS (2 JSQ + 1 SQ) -----

Q1 — Factual / JSQ
Sub-competency: Define an error on a faster payment
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: RTP error types — wrong amount, unintended receiver, duplicate
payment — are foundational definitions that a 2-year practitioner working with
real-time payment networks should recognize.

Stem:
  Which of the following is defined as a payment error on the RTP network?

Responses:
  A. A payment sent to an unintended receiving participant or receiver
  B. A payment made using an expired digital signature certificate
  C. A payment that exceeds the sending participant's internally configured
     transaction limit

Answer: A

Source citation:
  AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework, "Error on a
  faster payment" section, lines 9873–9881.

Sub-competency: Explain the liabilities and error resolution responsibilities

  payment with Accept Without Posting delay its resolution beyond midnight
  Eastern Time of the next standard business day?

     institution and is awaiting a response within the 20-business-day
