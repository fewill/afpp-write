Sub-competency 1 — Why onboarding programs manage risk
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
AFPP Handbook, lines 8193–8210:
  "There are a number of different risks related to Faster Payments,
  though legal and security risks generally originate from the account
  holder side. Proper onboarding of account holders is vital for
  financial institutions and third parties in order to minimize these
  risks... As Faster Payment methods, especially those that are
  irrevocable, are easier to abuse by fraudsters, the number of cases
  can increase significantly and thus the legal risks and costs along
  with them. Therefore, participants of Faster Payment systems ought to
  pay increased attention to applying fraud prevention and mitigation
  methodologies, including but not limited to, adequate customer
  onboarding procedures."

FedNow Service Operating Procedures, lines 309–319:
  Participants must maintain: (a) AML/sanctions compliance programs;
  (b) customer due diligence programs consistent with FinCEN standards;
  (c) reasonable procedures for screening customer information against
  current sanctions lists.

FedNow Service Operating Procedures, lines 330–339:
  Material or ongoing failures may lead to the Federal Reserve Banks
  disabling, suspending, or terminating a Participant's access to the
  FedNow Service — demonstrating that onboarding/compliance failures
  carry direct operational consequences.

Sub-competency 2 — Elements of a CIP
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
AFPP Handbook, lines 8214–8218:
  Under the USA PATRIOT Act, financial institutions are required to have
  a CIP in place to verify an account holder's identity and prevent
  fraudulent account openings and other financial crimes. Each
  institution must have a written CIP appropriate for its size and type
  of business, including minimum requirements set by the BSA.

AFPP Handbook, lines 8235–8242 (information to collect and verify):
  Collect name, date of birth, address, and identification number (e.g.,
  Social Security number for U.S. citizens). Review a government-issued
  form of identification with a photograph (valid passport or driver's
  license). FIs may also verify without documents by contacting the
  person, using independent verification services (credit reporting
  agencies), or checking references from other FIs.

AFPP Handbook, lines 8245–8249 (record keeping):
  Maintain records for five years after an account is closed. Retain
  descriptions of documents used for verification, methods used for
  verification in the absence of documents, and resolution of any
  substantive discrepancy discovered when verifying customer information.
  Cite: 31 CFR 1010.430.

AFPP Handbook, lines 8252–8257 (government list comparison):
  CIP must include procedures to determine whether the person appears on
  any government sanctions list (e.g., OFAC's Specially Designated
  Nationals (SDN) List). These checks must occur within a reasonable
  time following account opening. Accounts must be periodically checked
  against the OFAC SDN List thereafter.

Sub-competency 3 — CIP party responsibilities
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
AFPP Handbook, Table 55, lines 8265–8290:
  Financial Institutions:
    – Set appropriate CIP based on size and type of business
    – Obtain required customer information upon account opening
    – Verify identity through risk-based procedures
    – File a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) upon CIP failure
    – Comply with record keeping requirements
    – Cross-check registered accounts against government lists
    – Provide adequate notice before verification procedures
    – When relying on another FI, ensure it is regulated by a federal
      regulator and has adequate AML procedures
    – Ensure third-party providers adhere to the CIP
  Third Parties:
    – Carry out services to FIs according to the FI's CIP, regardless
      of data collection or management role
  Customers:
    – Share required information upon account opening
    – Update information upon changes

FedNow Service Operating Procedures, lines 2051–2056 (Sender FI duties
for RfP monitoring):
  Sender FIs must use risk-based procedures to track the volume of RfPs
  sent by customers to identify potential anomalous activity. They must
  review and incorporate any reports received regarding customer use of
  RfP. They must also have procedures to investigate anomalous activity,
  including inquiring with the relevant customer and taking remedial
  action to address misuse.

FedNow Service Operating Procedures, lines 2076–2081 (Receiver FI
duties before filing a warranty claim):
  If a Receiver FI believes an RfP was sent in breach of the RfP
  Warranty, it should inform the Sender FI of the allegedly breaching
  RfP. Before filing a claim, the Receiver FI must make a determination,
  based on facts collected through reasonable diligence, that the RfP
  Warranty was breached.

Sub-competency 4 — RfP additional requirements
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
AFPP Handbook, lines 8292–8306 (RTP):
  TCH requires participants that send Request for Payment messages to
  ensure the sender has a legitimate purpose to request funds:
    – Non-consumer sender: initiated for a current sale or transaction,
      or an amount that is due, owed, or otherwise agreed to be paid.
    – Consumer sender: initiated to a receiver who is known to the
      sender and who is reasonably expected to receive the request.

FedNow Service Operating Procedures, lines 2037–2047 (FedNow RfP
Warranty):
  RfPs must be sent only for legitimate purposes. For business customers:
  the RfP is not legitimate if sent for anything other than a current
  sale or transaction, or an amount due, owed, or previously agreed.
  For individual customers: an RfP is in breach if it is not reasonable
  for the receiver to have expected to receive it. Use of RfP contrary
  to applicable law (including UDAAP) would not constitute a legitimate
  purpose. Note: dissatisfaction with goods/services alone does not
  give rise to a breach of the RfP Warranty unless additional
  circumstances indicate it was not sent for a legitimate purpose.

FedNow Service Operating Procedures, lines 4819–4821 (zero-dollar RfP):
  The zero-dollar RfP market practice allows billers to assess whether
  customers are ready to receive and act upon RfPs before sending actual
  RfPs. Use is not required but is recommended to improve the overall
  biller/customer experience with RfP onboarding.

Q1 — Factual / JSQ

Sub-competency: Why onboarding programs manage risk
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Understanding that irrevocability makes faster payments
easier to abuse by fraudsters — making adequate onboarding a key fraud
prevention and legal risk mitigation tool — is foundational working
knowledge for a 2-year faster payments practitioner.

Stem:
  Why are customer onboarding programs considered especially important
  for managing risk in faster payment systems?

Responses:
  A. Because faster payment methods, especially those that are
     irrevocable, are easier for fraudsters to abuse, making adequate
     onboarding a key fraud prevention and legal risk mitigation tool
  B. Because faster payment systems require all participants to maintain
     a minimum customer base in order to sustain network liquidity and
     settlement coverage
  C. Because faster payment customers are exempt from standard credit
     review requirements and must be screened through separate
     onboarding-specific identity protocols

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 3 – Faster Payments Risk
Management, lines 8193–8210.

requires deeper regulatory knowledge beyond 2-year working knowledge of

  institution retain records of the information used to verify a
  customer's identity under its customer identification program?

     institution's general record retention policy for account

Q7 — Conditional / JSQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: RfP additional requirements
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The clear, single-rule condition — that a business customer's
RfP must relate to a current sale, transaction, or previously agreed
amount — is a straightforward warranty requirement a 2-year practitioner
handling RfP onboarding should be able to apply.

Stem:
  Under what condition does a business customer's use of Request for
  Payment fail to satisfy the legitimate purpose requirement of the RfP
  Warranty?

Responses:
  A. When the business customer sends a Request for Payment for an
     amount that is NOT related to a current sale, transaction, or a
     sum previously agreed to be paid by the receiving customer
  B. When the business customer sends a Request for Payment for an
     amount that exceeds the threshold established by the Sender
     financial institution's internally configured transaction
     monitoring system
  C. When the business customer sends a Request for Payment to a
     receiving customer who has NOT completed the zero-dollar Request
     for Payment readiness assessment recommended by the FedNow Service

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 3 – Faster Payments Risk
Management, lines 8292–8306. Also: FedNow Service Operating Procedures
(June 2025), lines 2037–2047.

Q8 — Conditional / SQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: CIP party responsibilities
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The specific conditions under which a financial institution
may rely on another institution's CIP procedures — requiring that the
other institution be federally regulated and have adequate AML
procedures — involves regulatory nuance about inter-institution CIP
reliance that requires 5+ years of compliance expertise to apply
reliably.

Stem:
  Under what condition may a financial institution rely on another
  financial institution's customer identification program procedures
  for a shared customer?

Responses:
  A. When both financial institutions have executed a formal CIP sharing
     agreement and submitted the agreement to their primary federal
     regulator for approval prior to account opening
  B. When the other financial institution is regulated by a federal
     financial regulator and has adequate anti-money laundering
     procedures in place
  C. When the customer has provided written consent authorizing the
     sharing of identity verification information between the two
     financial institutions prior to account opening

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 3 – Faster Payments Risk
Management, Table 55 – Faster Payments Stakeholder Responsibilities in
the Customer Identification Program, lines 8265–8290.

Q9 — Conditional / SQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: RfP additional requirements
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: Applying the individual-sender prong of the RfP Warranty —
that the receiver must be known to the consumer sender and reasonably
expected to receive the request — requires synthesis of the two-prong
legitimate-purpose test (business vs. consumer sender) and
distinguishing the consumer standard from the business standard, a
nuanced regulatory analysis characteristic of a 5-year practitioner.

Stem:
  Under what condition does a consumer sender's use of Request for
  Payment comply with the RfP Warranty's legitimate purpose requirement?

Responses:
  A. When the consumer sender confirms in writing to the Sender
     financial institution that the amount requested reflects a
     mutually agreed-upon obligation and attaches documentation of the
     underlying transaction
  B. When the consumer sender limits the Request for Payment to amounts
     that fall below the maximum dollar threshold established for
     consumer-initiated faster payments under applicable Federal Reserve
     guidelines
  C. When the Request for Payment is sent to a receiver who is known
     to the consumer sender and who is reasonably expected to receive
     the request from that sender

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 3 – Faster Payments Risk
Management, lines 8292–8306. Also: FedNow Service Operating Procedures
(June 2025), lines 2037–2047.
