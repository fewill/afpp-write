SECTION A: Definition and Overview of Overlay Services
  Source: AFPP Handbook, lines 8973–8994
  Section heading: "Faster Payments Value-Added Services" (Chapter 4)

  Key passage:
  "Overlay services are specialized offerings that enhance payment services by
  utilizing existing infrastructure to provide value-added services (VAS) to
  users... Overlay services include liquidity management, tokenization, biller
  directory(ies), fraud prevention, and customer directory(ies). These services
  benefit all participants in the payment ecosystem and facilitate the expansion
  and interoperability of instant payment services." (lines 8973–8980)

  Additional context (lines 1534–1540):
  "Value-added services (VAS), such as aliases to ease payment initiation or
  confirmation-of-payee, which helps prevent misdirected payments and fraud, can
  go a long way to make Faster Payments either more convenient, more trustworthy,
  or add some other type of value that provides end users with a reason to use
  these systems instead of other options."

SECTION B: Alias Directories
  Source: AFPP Handbook, lines 8513–8520, 8767–8785, 8994–9000

  Definition (lines 8513–8520):
  "Directories are databases of payment-related information and simplify payment
  routing by enabling payment initiation using social aliases such as email, cell
  phone number, etc... These aliases are linked to payment account information and
  replace the necessity of memorizing, sharing, or storing sensitive and
  complicated information, thereby facilitating payment initiation and assisting
  in fraud prevention."

  Two directory models (lines 8767–8779):
  - Centralized: maps alias to PSP and account number in a single step
  - Federated: maps alias to PSP only; PSP is responsible for mapping account
    information locally using its own directory (two-step process)

  US example (lines 8781–8785):
  "Zelle operates the largest Faster Payments alias directory for bank-initiated
  transactions in the United States."

  Sub-competency coverage: Identify types; list examples and characteristics

SECTION C: Request for Payment (RfP)
  Source: AFPP Handbook, lines 9001–9009; FedNow Operating Procedures, lines
  4812–4815 and 4856–4863

  AFPP Handbook definition (lines 9001–9009):
  "A Request for Payment is a standardized network message that payees can
  leverage to send electronic requests to payers through a faster payment network.
  Unlike direct debits, where the payment itself is initiated by the payee, the
  paying party controls when and if they will respond to an RfP request with a
  payment. The underlying payment is still a credit transfer even if the RfP
  itself is generated by the payee. RfP messages may include reconciliation data
  for bill and invoice presentation, allowing for easier presentation and
  information collection."

  FedNow participation condition (lines 4812–4815):
  "All Participants enabled for Customer Credit Transfers can send an RFP to a
  Receiver FI. The Receiver FI must have the appropriate FedNow Service
  participation type enabled to receive RFPs, i.e., Customer Credit Transfer Send
  and Receive with Receive Request for Payment."

  FedNow use cases (lines 4860–4863):
  RfPs support: payment or repayment of a debt between individuals; bill pay;
  tax or fee collection.

  Network availability: FedNow — Yes; RTP — Yes; Same Day ACH — No;
  Visa Direct — No; Mastercard Send — No.
  Source: Table 58, lines 9085–9214

  Sub-competency coverage: Identify types; list examples; describe how services
  support rails (conditional on FedNow participation type)

SECTION D: Confirmation-of-Payee (COP)
  Source: AFPP Handbook, lines 9010–9019, 1656–1669

  Definition (lines 9010–9019):
  "Confirmation-of-Payee (COP) is a service designed to cut down on misidentified
  or misdirected payments by providing the sending party with the name associated
  with the beneficiary account prior to a payment being initiated. COP helps
  inspire user trust by providing identity verification services, enhancing overall
  transparency, and cutting down on error rates."

  Fraud prevention context (lines 1656–1669):
  COP reduces errors from inputting wrong account information and helps prevent
  Authorized Push Payment (APP) fraud by comparing the name provided by the sender
  against the name associated with the recipient's account.

  Network availability: Visa Direct — Yes; Mastercard Send — Yes;
  FedNow — No (possible through Zelle); Same Day ACH — No (possible through Zelle)
  Source: Table 58, lines 9085–9214

  Sub-competency coverage: List examples and characteristics

SECTION E: Tokenization
  Source: AFPP Handbook, lines 8521–8539, 9020–9024

  Definition (lines 9020–9024):
  "Tokenization is an available service to enhance the security of financial
  transactions. This protects users from fraud by eliminating the need to transmit
  sensitive information in each transaction."

  Mechanism (lines 8521–8539):
  A Token Service Provider replaces sensitive payment data with a unique, random
  string of characters (a "token") that is meaningless to bad actors. Authenticated
  participants can present tokens to the Token Service Provider to retrieve the
  original sensitive information.

  Network availability in RTP: "Yes, optional via Secure Token Exchange service"
  Source: Table 58, lines 9085–9214

  Sub-competency coverage: List examples and characteristics; conditional on
  network participation

SECTION F: Fraud Scoring
  Source: AFPP Handbook, lines 9044–9056

  Definition and mechanics (lines 9044–9056):
  "Fraud scoring provides insights at the central infrastructure- and FI-level by
  employing advanced data analytics to assess a transaction based on various risk
  indicators. Fraud scoring involves analyzing transaction frequency, amount,
  location, end user behavior patterns, and other data points to determine whether
  a transaction is likely fraudulent in nature."

  Why infrastructure operators are best suited:
  "Faster Payment infrastructure operators are best suited to manage fraud scoring
  systems due to their ability to monitor a large volume of payments from a
  centralized view. Operators can then more easily identify patterns and anomalies,
  enabling the development of advanced fraud detection algorithms, and allowing for
  the swift communication of risk alerts to FIs via the network's
  telecommunication rails." (lines 9049–9056)

  Sub-competency coverage: List examples and characteristics; describe how services
  support rails

SECTION G: Digital IDs
  Source: AFPP Handbook, lines 9025–9043

  Definition and risk (lines 9025–9043):
  "Digital IDs are another trust-enhancing value-added service that allows end
  users to verify their identity in a digital environment, preventing unauthorized
  parties from initiating payments... The rise of generative AI, however, poses a
  risk for digital IDs that rely on facial or voice recognition." (lines 9041–9043)

  Sub-competency coverage: List examples and characteristics; conditional risk

SECTION H: Account Debit/Credit Notifications (FedNow)
  Source: FedNow Operating Procedures, lines 5469–5472

  "The FedNow Service offers account debit/credit notifications on an opt-in basis
  for Correspondents that have enabled a Participant Profile. The FedNow Service
  uses an account credit/debit notification (camt.054) message to provide these
  Correspondents with real-time notice of a value payment (pacs.008, pacs.009,
  pacs.004) that was processed and settled by the FedNow Service."

  Sub-competency coverage: Describe how value-added services support faster
  payments rails (FedNow-specific)

----- FACTUAL QUESTIONS (3) -----

Q1 — Factual (Types of overlay/VAS)
Sub-competency: Identify types of value-added and overlay services

Stem:
  Which type of overlay service enables payment initiation using a social alias
  such as an email address or mobile phone number?

Responses:
  A. An alias directory
  B. A fraud scoring system
  C. A tokenization service

Answer: A

Rationale: Lines 8513–8520 and 8994–9000 describe alias directories as the
service that links social aliases (email, phone number) to payment account
information to enable payment initiation. Fraud scoring assesses transaction
risk; tokenization replaces sensitive data with tokens — neither enables
alias-based initiation.

Source citation:
  AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 4 – Technology Considerations for
  Enabling Faster Payments, "Alias Directories" subsection, lines 8994–9000.

Q2 — Factual (Network availability of RfP)
Sub-competency: List examples and characteristics of overlay services

Stem:
  On which two faster payment networks is the Request for Payment overlay
  service available?

Responses:
  A. Same Day ACH and FedNow
  B. FedNow and the RTP network
  C. Visa Direct and the RTP network

Answer: B

Rationale: Table 58 (lines 9085–9214) shows the Request for Payment service
as available on FedNow and the RTP network. Same Day ACH, Visa Direct, and
Mastercard Send do not offer the service.

Source citation:
  AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 4 – Technology Considerations for
  Enabling Faster Payments, Table 58: "Faster Payment Overlay Services in
  the US," lines 9085–9214.

Q3 — Factual (COP purpose)
Sub-competency: List examples and characteristics of overlay services

Stem:
  Which action does a confirmation-of-payee service perform to help mitigate
  misdirected payments and Authorized Push Payment fraud?

Responses:
  A. It replaces the recipient's account number with a unique token before
     the payment is routed to the receiving financial institution
  B. It sends an electronic request to the payer through the faster payment
     network asking the payer to authorize the transaction
  C. It provides the sending party with the name associated with the
     beneficiary account before a payment is initiated

Answer: C

Rationale: Lines 9010–9019 define confirmation-of-payee as a service that
provides the sending party with the beneficiary account name prior to
initiation. Response A describes tokenization; response B describes a Request
for Payment.

Source citation:
  AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 4 – Technology Considerations for
  Enabling Faster Payments, "Confirmation-of-Payee (COP)" subsection,
  lines 9010–9019.

----- PROCEDURAL QUESTIONS (3) -----

Q4 — Procedural (Federated vs. centralized directory)
Sub-competency: List examples and characteristics of overlay services

Stem:
  How does a federated alias directory process a payment alias differently from
  a centralized alias directory?

Responses:
  A. A federated directory maps an alias to the payment service provider, and
     the payment service provider maps the alias to the account number using
     its own local directory
  B. A federated directory maps an alias directly to both the payment service
     provider and the account number in a single operation
  C. A federated directory replaces the alias with a unique token before
     routing the payment to the receiving financial institution

Answer: A

Rationale: Lines 8767–8779 describe the two-step process of a federated
directory: the directory maps alias to PSP, and the PSP maps alias to account
locally. Response B describes how a centralized directory works. Response C
describes tokenization, not a directory model.

Source citation:
  AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 4 – Technology Considerations for
  Enabling Faster Payments, "Centralized and Federated Directories"
  subsection, lines 8767–8779.

Q5 — Procedural (Fraud scoring — how it supports rails)
Sub-competency: Describe how value-added and overlay services support faster
payments rails

Stem:
  How does a centralized fraud scoring service operated by a faster payment
  infrastructure operator support financial institution risk management?

Responses:
  A. It requires financial institutions to resubmit flagged payments through
     a secondary clearing cycle before settlement is completed
  B. It identifies patterns and anomalies across the network and communicates
     risk alerts to financial institutions via the network's communication
     rails
  C. It replaces the financial institution's internal fraud monitoring
     procedures by processing all transaction screening at the infrastructure
     level

Answer: B

Rationale: Lines 9049–9056 describe the mechanism: operators monitor a large
volume of payments centrally to identify patterns and anomalies and send risk
alerts to FIs via the network's telecommunication rails. The alerts supplement,
not replace, FI-level monitoring. There is no secondary clearing cycle
described in the source.

Source citation:
  AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 4 – Technology Considerations for
  Enabling Faster Payments, "Fraud Scoring" subsection, lines 9049–9056.

---

Q6 — Procedural (FedNow account notification service)
Sub-competency: Describe how value-added and overlay services support faster
payments rails

Stem:
  Which message type does the FedNow Service use to provide a correspondent
  with real-time notice that a value payment was processed and settled on
  behalf of a respondent participant?

Responses:
  A. pacs.008
  B. pain.013
  C. camt.054

Answer: C

Rationale: FedNow Operating Procedures lines 5469–5472 state that the FedNow
Service uses a camt.054 (account credit/debit notification) message for this
purpose. pacs.008 is the customer credit transfer message; pain.013 is the
Request for Payment message.

Source citation:
  Federal Reserve Banks. FedNow Service Operating Procedures (June 24, 2025).
  Section 16.3 "Account Credit/Debit Notification (camt.054)," lines 5469–5472.

Q7 — Conditional (Digital ID security risk)
Sub-competency: List examples and characteristics of overlay services

Stem:
  Under what condition does a digital ID overlay service become vulnerable
  to circumvention as a method of verifying a payment initiator's identity?

Responses:
  A. When the service relies on facial or voice recognition and is exposed
     to threats from generative artificial intelligence
  B. When the service is issued by a government entity rather than embedded
     in a private financial institution's mobile banking application
  C. When the service is used to verify identity across multiple faster
     payment networks that do not share a common authentication framework

Answer: A

Rationale: Lines 9041–9043 state: "The rise of generative AI, however, poses
a risk for digital IDs that rely on facial or voice recognition." The source
does not identify issuing entity or multi-network use as the vulnerability.

Source citation:
  AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 4 – Technology Considerations for
  Enabling Faster Payments, "Digital IDs" subsection, lines 9041–9043.

Q8 — Conditional (FedNow RfP receive condition)
Sub-competency: Describe how value-added and overlay services support faster
payments rails

Stem:
  Under what condition is a FedNow participant able to receive a Request for
  Payment message from another participant?

Responses:
  A. The participant has established a negative list profile with the FedNow
     Service for the relevant routing and account number pairs
  B. The participant has the Customer Credit Transfer Send and Receive with
     Receive Request for Payment participation type enabled
  C. The participant has designated a service provider to present Request for
     Payment messages to its end customers on its behalf

Answer: B

Rationale: FedNow Operating Procedures lines 4812–4815 state: "The Receiver
FI must have the appropriate FedNow Service participation type enabled to
receive RFPs, i.e., Customer Credit Transfer Send and Receive with Receive
Request for Payment." The negative list relates to fraud controls, and service
provider designation is not the condition cited.

Source citation:
  Federal Reserve Banks. FedNow Service Operating Procedures (June 24, 2025).
  Section 16.1 "Request for Payment (RFP) (pain.013)," lines 4812–4815.

---

Q9 — Conditional (RTP tokenization availability)
Sub-competency: List examples and characteristics of overlay services

Stem:
  Under what condition is tokenization available as an overlay service for
  participants in the RTP network?

Responses:
  A. When the participant enables the Request for Payment participation type
     and connects through a certified service provider
  B. When the participant maintains a Current Prefunded Position sufficient
     to cover the value of each tokenized transaction
  C. When the participant opts in to the Secure Token Exchange service
     offered by the network

Answer: C

Rationale: Table 58 (lines 9085–9214) indicates that tokenization on the RTP
network is available as "Yes, optional via Secure Token Exchange service."
The other responses describe prefunding and RfP conditions that are unrelated
to tokenization availability.

Source citation:
  AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 4 – Technology Considerations for
  Enabling Faster Payments, Table 58: "Faster Payment Overlay Services in
  the US," lines 9085–9214.
