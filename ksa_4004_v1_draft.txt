Draft Test Questions

Q1 — Factual

Which type of overlay service enables payment initiation using a social alias
such as an email address or mobile phone number?

A. An alias directory
B. A fraud scoring system
C. A tokenization service

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 4 – Technology Considerations
for Enabling Faster Payments, "Alias Directories" subsection, lines 8994–9000.

Q2 — Factual

On which two faster payment networks is the Request for Payment overlay
service available?

A. Same Day ACH and FedNow
B. FedNow and the RTP network
C. Visa Direct and the RTP network

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 4 – Technology Considerations
for Enabling Faster Payments, Table 58: "Faster Payment Overlay Services in
the US," lines 9085–9214.

Q3 — Factual

Which action does a confirmation-of-payee service perform to help mitigate
misdirected payments and Authorized Push Payment fraud?

A. It replaces the recipient's account number with a unique token before
   the payment is routed to the receiving financial institution
B. It sends an electronic request to the payer through the faster payment
   network asking the payer to authorize the transaction
C. It provides the sending party with the name associated with the
   beneficiary account before a payment is initiated

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 4 – Technology Considerations
for Enabling Faster Payments, "Confirmation-of-Payee (COP)" subsection,
lines 9010–9019.

Q4 — Procedural

How does a federated alias directory process a payment alias differently from
a centralized alias directory?

A. A federated directory maps an alias to the payment service provider, and
   the payment service provider maps the alias to the account number using
   its own local directory
B. A federated directory maps an alias directly to both the payment service
   provider and the account number in a single operation
C. A federated directory replaces the alias with a unique token before
   routing the payment to the receiving financial institution

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 4 – Technology Considerations
for Enabling Faster Payments, "Centralized and Federated Directories"
subsection, lines 8767–8779.

Q5 — Procedural

How does a centralized fraud scoring service operated by a faster payment
infrastructure operator support financial institution risk management?

A. It requires financial institutions to resubmit flagged payments through
   a secondary clearing cycle before settlement is completed
B. It identifies patterns and anomalies across the network and communicates
   risk alerts to financial institutions via the network's communication
   rails
C. It replaces the financial institution's internal fraud monitoring
   procedures by processing all transaction screening at the infrastructure
   level

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 4 – Technology Considerations
for Enabling Faster Payments, "Fraud Scoring" subsection, lines 9049–9056.

Q6 — Procedural

Which message type does the FedNow Service use to provide a correspondent
with real-time notice that a value payment was processed and settled on
behalf of a respondent participant?

A. pacs.008
B. pain.013
C. camt.054

Answer: C

Source: Federal Reserve Banks. FedNow Service Operating Procedures
(June 24, 2025). Section 16.3 "Account Credit/Debit Notification
(camt.054)," lines 5469–5472.

Q7 — Conditional

Under what condition does a digital ID overlay service become vulnerable
to circumvention as a method of verifying a payment initiator's identity?

A. When the service relies on facial or voice recognition and is exposed
   to threats from generative artificial intelligence
B. When the service is issued by a government entity rather than embedded
   in a private financial institution's mobile banking application
C. When the service is used to verify identity across multiple faster
   payment networks that do not share a common authentication framework

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 4 – Technology Considerations
for Enabling Faster Payments, "Digital IDs" subsection, lines 9041–9043.

Q8 — Conditional

Under what condition is a FedNow participant able to receive a Request for
Payment message from another participant?

A. The participant has established a negative list profile with the FedNow
   Service for the relevant routing and account number pairs
B. The participant has the Customer Credit Transfer Send and Receive with
   Receive Request for Payment participation type enabled
C. The participant has designated a service provider to present Request for
   Payment messages to its end customers on its behalf

Answer: B

Source: Federal Reserve Banks. FedNow Service Operating Procedures
(June 24, 2025). Section 16.1 "Request for Payment (RFP) (pain.013),"
lines 4812–4815.

Q9 — Conditional

Under what condition is tokenization available as an overlay service for
participants in the RTP network?

A. When the participant enables the Request for Payment participation type
   and connects through a certified service provider
B. When the participant maintains a Current Prefunded Position sufficient
   to cover the value of each tokenized transaction
C. When the participant opts in to the Secure Token Exchange service
   offered by the network

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 4 – Technology Considerations
for Enabling Faster Payments, Table 58: "Faster Payment Overlay Services in
the US," lines 9085–9214.
