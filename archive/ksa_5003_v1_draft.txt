KSA 5003: Error Resolution Protections, Rights, and Liabilities of Payers and Payees
Draft Test Questions
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q1 — Factual

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: RTP error types — wrong amount, unintended receiver, and duplicate
payment — are foundational definitions that a 2-year practitioner working with
real-time payment networks should recognize.

Which of the following is defined as a payment error on the RTP network?

A. A payment sent to an unintended receiving participant or receiver
B. A payment made using an expired digital signature certificate
C. A payment that exceeds the sending participant's internally configured
   transaction limit

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework, "Error
on a faster payment" section, lines 9873–9881.

Q2 — Factual

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Knowing that Regulation E governs consumer electronic fund transfers
is fundamental regulatory knowledge expected of a 2-year payments practitioner.

Which type of payment transaction does Regulation E govern with respect to
error resolution protections?

A. Commercial funds transfers between businesses processed through a faster
   payment network
B. Electronic fund transfers initiated to debit or credit a consumer account
C. Wire transfers processed between financial institutions under Regulation J

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Applicability of error resolution protections under Regulation E and UCC4A,"
lines 9883–9884. Also: FFIEC IT Examination Handbook: Retail Payment Systems
Booklet, lines 521–525.

Q3 — Factual

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: The 90-day UCC Article 4A notification window for payers is a
specific regulatory detail that requires deeper knowledge to distinguish from
the more commonly recognized 60-day Regulation E window.

Under UCC Article 4A, within how many days of receiving payment confirmation
must a payer notify its financial institution of a misdirected payment to avoid
liability for the full amount sent?

A. 60 days
B. 120 days
C. 90 days

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework, "Payer
liability for a misdirected payment" section, lines 9920–9922.

Q4 — Procedural

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: The 10-business-day investigation requirement under Regulation E is
a standard compliance procedure that a 2-year payments practitioner applies in
consumer error resolution workflows.

Under Regulation E, within how many business days must a financial institution
complete its investigation after a consumer reports a payment error?

A. 10 business days
B. 3 business days
C. 45 calendar days

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework, Table
62: "Liabilities and Error Resolution Responsibilities of Financial Institutions
by Payment Rail," lines 9947–9950.

Q5 — Procedural

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: The 60-calendar-day timeframe for FedNow return requests is an
operational parameter that a practitioner working with FedNow should know as
a standard procedure.

According to the FedNow Service Operating Procedures, within how many calendar
days of a credit transfer settling should a participant send a return request
for that payment?

A. 30 calendar days
B. 60 calendar days
C. 95 calendar days

Answer: B

Source: Federal Reserve Banks. FedNow Service Operating Procedures
(June 24, 2025), lines 4126–4131 and 4219–4220.

Q6 — Procedural

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: The provisional credit extension mechanism — requiring action within
10 business days and consumer notice within 2 business days — is a nuanced
Regulation E procedure that demands deeper regulatory expertise beyond working
practitioner knowledge.

Under Regulation E, how may a financial institution extend its error
investigation period from 10 business days to 45 calendar days?

A. By filing a notice of extension with the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau
   and informing the consumer within 5 business days of the extension
B. By transferring the investigation to the sending financial institution and
   suspending consumer account activity until the matter is resolved
C. By providing provisional funds to the consumer within the original 10
   business days and advising the consumer within 2 business days of the
   provisional credit

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework, Table
62: "Liabilities and Error Resolution Responsibilities of Financial Institutions
by Payment Rail," lines 9950–9953.

Q7 — Conditional

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: Applying the consumer/commercial distinction to determine which
regulatory framework governs error resolution is a foundational analytical skill
for a 2-year payments compliance practitioner.

Under what condition does UCC Article 4A — rather than Regulation E — govern
the error resolution process for a faster payment?

A. The payment is a commercial transaction not involving a consumer account
B. The payment is processed through a network that has not incorporated
   Regulation E protections into its operating rules
C. The payment involves an account held for personal, family, or household
   purposes

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Applicability of error resolution protections under Regulation E and UCC4A,"
lines 9882–9902. Also: FFIEC IT Examination Handbook: Retail Payment Systems
Booklet, lines 571–575.

Q8 — Conditional

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: Knowing the specific UCC Article 4A remedy for a wrong-beneficiary
error — and recognizing it applies only to non-consumer transactions — requires
synthesis of regulatory frameworks characteristic of a 5-year practitioner.

Under what condition is a receiving financial institution required to recover
and return the full amount of a payment order under UCC Article 4A?

A. The payment was processed through a network using the ISO 20022 messaging
   standard and the sender submitted a return request within 60 calendar days
B. The payment order was debited to a beneficiary different from the one
   specified in the original payment order
C. The payment was accepted without posting and the receiving financial
   institution failed to resolve the payment status by the next standard
   business day

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework, Table
62: "Liabilities and Error Resolution Responsibilities of Financial Institutions
by Payment Rail," lines 9956–9962.

Q9 — Conditional

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The narrow exception allowing delayed ACWP resolution — and the
specific pending status response it requires — demands deep operational and
regulatory knowledge of FedNow procedures beyond a 2-year practitioner's
working knowledge.

Under what condition may a FedNow participant that has responded to an instant
payment with Accept Without Posting delay its resolution beyond midnight
Eastern Time of the next standard business day?

A. The participant has submitted a return request to the sending financial
   institution and is awaiting a response within the 20-business-day response
   window
B. The participant has insufficient funds in its Federal Reserve Master Account
   to provide provisional credit to the consumer within the required timeframe
C. The participant continues to be concerned that providing or returning the
   payment would be impermissible under applicable law and has provided a
   pending status response to the FedNow Service by the deadline

Answer: C

Source: Federal Reserve Banks. FedNow Service Operating Procedures
(June 24, 2025), lines 3279–3291.
