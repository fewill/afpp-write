Q1 — Factual / JSQ

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Recognizing the FPC's defining characteristic of an overlay
service — that it is owned and operated by a third party and deployed
on basic infrastructure — is working knowledge for a 2-year faster
payments practitioner.

According to the Faster Payments Council Glossary, which of the
following best describes an overlay service?

  A. A tailored, value-adding payment service owned and operated by a
     third party that is deployed on basic infrastructure to deliver
     value to a specific group of subscribers and end users
  B. A network-level payment routing service operated by a central
     infrastructure provider to support interoperability between faster
     payment rails
  C. A proprietary payment application developed by a financial
     institution to enable customers to initiate payments using a
     mobile device

Answer: A

Source: Glossary of Terms. Faster Payments Council. "Overlay Service"
definition, lines 619–620.

Q2 — Factual / JSQ

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Understanding that a payment directory service uses aliases
to avoid sharing sensitive account credentials — its defining functional
characteristic — is working knowledge for a 2-year faster payments
practitioner.

What is the primary function of a payment directory service in faster
payment systems?

  A. To store encrypted transaction records that participants can query
     to verify the settlement status of prior payments
  B. To enable payments without requiring sensitive financial account
     credentials to be shared between payee and payer, by using an
     alias associated with payment information
  C. To authenticate participants before connecting to the faster
     payment network by verifying routing and account number pairs

Answer: B

Source: Glossary of Terms. Faster Payments Council. "Payment Directory
Service" definition, lines 646–647.

Q3 — Factual / SQ

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Knowing that the FedNow Service is the only U.S. faster
payment rail that offers a liquidity management overlay service, while
the RTP network and others do not, requires detailed knowledge of the
rail-by-rail overlay availability matrix beyond 2-year working
knowledge.

According to the AFPP Handbook, on which U.S. faster payment rail is
a liquidity management overlay service available?

  A. Same Day ACH, because the Nacha Operating Rules require
     participants to access liquidity management tools to manage
     settlement exposure between processing windows
  B. The RTP network, because The Clearing House designed its
     prefunding model to integrate with a centralized liquidity
     management system for Funding Participants
  C. FedNow, because the FedNow Service provides participants with
     tools to make internal adjustments and monitor cash flows to
     support their liquidity needs

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 4 – Technology
Considerations for Enabling Faster Payments, Table 58 – Faster Payment
Overlay Services in the US, lines 9085–9211. Also: "Liquidity
Management" subsection, lines 9057–9064.

Q4 — Procedural / JSQ

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: Knowing that a Request for Payment message contains
remittance information enabling straight-through processing and invoice
matching is standard working knowledge for a 2-year practitioner
handling business payment products.

How does a Request for Payment message support the billing and
reconciliation process for business users of faster payment networks?

  A. By including remittance information in the message itself,
     enabling straight-through processing and easier matching of
     payments to invoices
  B. By routing the payment through a secondary verification cycle that
     confirms invoice amounts before settlement is initiated by the
     sending financial institution
  C. By triggering an automatic account debit from the payer's account
     upon acceptance, eliminating the need for a separate credit
     transfer message

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 4 – Technology
Considerations for Enabling Faster Payments, "Request for Payment"
subsection, lines 9003–9009.

Q5 — Procedural / JSQ

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: Understanding that liquidity management overlay services
mitigate service interruptions by providing cash flow visibility and
enabling proactive transfers is standard operational knowledge for a
2-year faster payments practitioner.

How does a liquidity management overlay service help faster payment
participants mitigate the risk of service interruptions?

  A. By automating settlement adjustments across multiple faster
     payment networks simultaneously to prevent overdrafts at any
     single network
  B. By providing insights into participants' liquidity positions and
     enabling proactive transfers to prevent insufficient funds before
     service interruptions occur
  C. By suspending outgoing payments from participants whose available
     funds fall below the minimum threshold established by the central
     infrastructure operator

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 4 – Technology
Considerations for Enabling Faster Payments, "Liquidity Management"
subsection, lines 9057–9064.

Q6 — Procedural / SQ

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: The specific mechanism by which ISO 20022 enables value-
added service innovation on the RTP network — allowing participants to
develop new products without customized development and aligning with
global markets — is a technical architecture concept requiring 5+ years
of expertise to understand and apply.

How does the RTP network's use of the ISO 20022 standard enable
participants to introduce new value-added services?

  A. By requiring all participants to complete a certification process
     for each new value-added service before deployment to ensure
     consistent implementation across the network
  B. By restricting value-added service development to The Clearing
     House and its designated third-party service providers to maintain
     network security and operational standards
  C. By allowing participants and service providers to develop and
     introduce new products without requiring customized development,
     enabling the U.S. market to move in tandem with global markets

Answer: C

Source: Introduction to the RTP System (October 2020). The Clearing
House. Section 2.6, "RTP and ISO 20022 Standards," page 20.

Q7 — Conditional / JSQ

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The clear, single-rule condition — that a Remittance Advice
is sent separately when remittance data does not fit in the Credit
Transfer message fields — is a straightforward characteristic of RTP
messaging that a 2-year practitioner handling business payment products
should be able to apply.

Under what condition may a payer's financial institution send a
Remittance Advice message separately from the Credit Transfer on the
RTP network?

  A. When the remittance information associated with the payment does
     NOT fit into the limited remittance data fields available within
     the Credit Transfer message
  B. When the payee's financial institution requests additional payment
     details through a Request for Information message within 24 hours
     of receiving the Credit Transfer
  C. When the payer's financial institution determines that the payment
     amount exceeds the threshold at which the network requires
     enhanced remittance data for compliance reporting

Answer: A

Source: Introduction to the RTP System (October 2020). The Clearing
House. Section 3.1, "Credit Transfer Event," pages 23–24.

Q8 — Conditional / SQ

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: Distinguishing an open alias directory from a private
directory based on industry-accepted governance, access standards, and
the aggregation of private directory entries requires nuanced knowledge
of directory service architecture beyond 2-year working knowledge.

Under what condition does an alias directory qualify as an open alias
directory rather than a private directory, according to the Faster
Payments Council Glossary?

  A. When the directory is operated by a central infrastructure
     provider and all registered participants are required to
     contribute alias entries as a condition of network participation
  B. When the directory is available to all approved parties under
     industry-accepted governance, with access controlled via industry-
     developed standards, and aggregates entries from private
     directories
  C. When the directory uses a federated architecture in which each
     participating financial institution maintains its own local alias
     database that synchronizes with a shared central repository

Answer: B

Source: Glossary of Terms. Faster Payments Council. "Open Alias
Directory" definition, lines 604–607. Also: "Private Directory"
definition, lines 706–707.

Q9 — Conditional / SQ

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The specific condition under which an exception handling
overlay service initiates a fund retrieval or return — requiring
synthesis of workflow design, predetermined response logic, and the
integration of exception management with faster payment operations —
requires multi-concept analysis characteristic of a 5-year practitioner.

Under what condition does an exception handling overlay service initiate
a fund retrieval or return workflow in a faster payment system?

  A. When the payer's financial institution submits a dispute request
     within the applicable return timeframe and the receiving financial
     institution confirms the funds have NOT been applied to the
     beneficiary account
  B. When the faster payment system detects a duplicate transaction and
     automatically reverses the most recent payment before settlement
     is finalized in the next processing cycle
  C. When an exception has been identified or reported and the
     predefined workflow established to resolve it includes fund
     retrieval or return as a predetermined response option

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 4 – Technology
Considerations for Enabling Faster Payments, "Exceptions Handling"
subsection, lines 9067–9072.
