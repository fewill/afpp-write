SECTION A: Required Agreements by Payment Rail
  Source: AFPP Handbook, lines 9982–10141 (Table 63 and detail sections)
  Section heading: Chapter 5 – Governance Framework

  Same Day ACH (lines 10067–10084):
  - Origination agreements (ODFI ↔ originator): bind originator to the Nacha
    Operating Rules; authorize ODFI to receive transactions on originator's
    behalf; specify transaction limitations; include termination and audit
    compliance rights.
  - Authorization agreements (originator ↔ receiver): permit receipt of ACH
    entries; terms vary by consumer vs. corporate entity and relationship.
  - ACH Operator agreements (ODFI/RDFI ↔ ACH Operator): establish routing
    numbers and settlement arrangements.

  RTP Network (lines 10087–10094):
  - RTP Participant Agreement and Indemnity: establishes terms and conditions
    for participation; requires adherence to RTP rules, security protocols,
    and transaction processing guidelines; participants agree to indemnify TCH.
  - Designation of Third Party Service Provider Agreement: designates third
    party to act on participant's behalf; outlines scope of services; ensures
    RTP Network standard compliance.

  FedNow (lines 10097–10109):
  - FedNow Service Security Procedure Agreement: references Operating Circular
    8; governs security procedures for FedNow access and Reserve Banks' actions
    on authenticated instructions.
  - Service Provider Agreement: authorizes designated entity to perform
    specific services (sending/receiving credit transfers, settlement management,
    liquidity management transfers); ensures service provider adherence to
    FedNow standards.

  Mastercard Send (lines 10112–10121):
  - Digital Activity Agreement: commits users to rules on digital participation;
    covers sponsorship rights, obligations, responsibilities, usage abilities.
  - Program Service Agreement: reflects customer responsibilities for management
    and operating policies per Mastercard standards.
  - Payment Transfer Agreement: establishes rule compliance, transaction
    responsibility, authorization requirements, information provision
    obligations, privacy, and data security requirements.

  Visa Direct (lines 10124–10141):
  - Terms and Conditions Agreement: outlines rights and responsibilities of
    developers, FIs, and merchants/consumers; defines parameters for API usage;
    covers push and pull transfer usage; grants limited license with
    distribution and sublicensing restrictions; Visa reserves right to regulate
    API calls and modify methods.

  Also: FFIEC booklet (lines 2474–2483):
  Nacha requires ODFIs to have written agreements with third-party senders
  specifying compliance with Nacha rules and applicable laws, including prior
  approval rights, dollar limits, restriction on file corrections, and
  requirement that third-party senders warrant originator identity.

SECTION B: Required Disclosures by Payment Rail
  Source: AFPP Handbook, lines 10027–10203 (Table 64 and detail sections)

  Same Day ACH (lines 10145–10152):
  - ODFIs must disclose nested third-party relationships.
  - ACH Operators must disclose designated data related to entries to Nacha.
  - All participants must disclose transaction data retention policies.
  - Comprehensive disclosure of pricing, exception handling, and operational
    information to participants.

  RTP Network (lines 10155–10164):
  - TCH must disclose: pricing, exceptions handling, policies, practices,
    standards to participants.
  - Participants must disclose to customers: pricing, standards, rules for RTP
    payments, exceptions processing.
  - Funding participants must disclose relationships with non-funding
    participants to TCH and furnish reconciliation reports.
  - TCH provides daily reconciliation reports to participants.
  - Participants must provide customers with necessary Payment Message
    information per RTP Technical Specifications.

  FedNow (lines 10167–10176):
  - FedNow participants allow Reserve Banks to utilize and disclose transaction
    records and related information for directory services, fraud services, and
    transaction enforcement.
  - FedNow participants must affirm they have provided all requisite disclosures
    and obtained necessary consents/permissions for Reserve Bank usage.
  - Source: Operating Circular 1 (Account Relationships) and Operating
    Circular 8.

  Mastercard Send (lines 10186–10191):
  - Institutions must receive required standards, privacy policies, fees, and
    pricing from Mastercard before becoming customers.
  - Participating FIs must disclose customer transaction activity reports to
    Mastercard and disclose all fees to customers in compliance with applicable
    laws.

  Visa Direct (lines 10194–10203):
  - Originators must furnish customers with comprehensive Terms and Conditions
    before Visa Direct service use.
  - Originators must regularly update Terms and Conditions to encompass
    necessary legal/industry disclosures; Visa reserves right to request copies
    for compliance verification.
  - Originators must provide senders with transparent fee information; senders
    must be allowed to consent to fees or cancel the transaction.
  - Visa Core Rules are disclosed to all participants with required compliance.

SECTION C: Warranties and Indemnities by Rail
  Source: AFPP Handbook, lines 10204–10270

  Same Day ACH (lines 10207–10216):
  - ODFI indemnifies RDFIs and ACH Operators for claims, losses, liabilities,
    and expenses stemming from breaches of ODFI warranties under the Nacha
    Operating Rules.
  - RDFI indemnifies ODFIs and ACH Operators for breaches of warranty under
    Section 1.2 of the Nacha Operating Rules.
  - Gateway operators assume the same warranties as ODFIs under the Nacha
    Operating Rules. (line 10215–10216)

  RTP Network (lines 10219–10228):
  - Participants agree to mutual indemnification for transactions facilitated
    via the RTP Network and pre-funded account balances used.
  - TCH is only liable in cases of its own gross negligence or intentional
    misconduct; amount limited to what is directly attributable to TCH.
  - TCH shall not be held liable for fraud except where deemed negligent.

  FedNow (lines 10238–10251):
  - Reserve Banks' liability is limited to losses directly resulting from
    willful misconduct or failure to exercise ordinary care. (OC8 framework)
  - Reserve Banks are NOT liable for special or consequential damages,
    regardless of foreseeability or prior notification. (lines 10241–10242)
  - Processing liability: limited to the amount of any fee paid for the
    message in cases of failure to exercise ordinary care or good faith.
  - Warranty disclaimers: Reserve Banks disclaim all warranties (express or
    implied) concerning accuracy, merchantability, fitness for particular
    purpose, or title of third-party products or services.
  - FedNow participants must indemnify and safeguard Reserve Banks against
    claims, losses, costs, and expenses from failure to fulfill OC8 obligations.

  Also: FedNow Operating Procedures (lines 2038–2056):
  - RFP Warranty: participants warrant that RFPs are sent only for legitimate
    purposes (as defined in OC8). Sender FIs must monitor RFP use for abuse
    (risk-based procedures to track volume and anomalous activity).
  - Warranty breach claim: Receiver FI submits camt.056 with WNTB reason code
    within 95 calendar days of credit transfer settlement.

  Mastercard Send (lines 10254–10261):
  - Each customer must indemnify and hold harmless Mastercard, its subsidiaries,
    directors, officers, and agents from claims arising from the customer's acts
    or omissions.
  - Mastercard disclaims all warranties regarding its systems.
  - Mastercard's total liability is capped at: compensation received from
    the customer OR USD 250,000, whichever is less, unless prohibited by law.
  - This limitation is a fundamental aspect of Mastercard Send standards.

  Visa Direct (lines 10264–10270):
  - Visa assumes liability solely for loss or misdirection of funds in
    push-to-account Original Credit Transactions when issues are directly
    attributable to Visa and within limitations in Visa Core Rules.
  - Visa disclaims liability for any indirect, incidental, consequential, or
    special damages, including loss of revenue, profits, or business
    opportunities, regardless of prior knowledge.
  - Visa holds no liability for actions or omissions of downstream entities
    including partners, banks, and processing schemes.

  Also: FFIEC booklet (lines 2493–2496):
  Third-party senders must warrant to ODFIs that originators will assume their
  responsibilities under Nacha rules and that the third-party sender assumes
  the liabilities of the ODFI.

----- FACTUAL QUESTIONS (2 JSQ + 1 SQ) -----

Q1 — Factual / JSQ
Sub-competency: Identify the required agreements for each payment rail
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: The purpose of an origination agreement in Same Day ACH — binding
the originator to network rules and authorizing the ODFI — is standard working
knowledge for a 2-year ACH practitioner.

Stem:
  Which of the following describes a primary purpose of an origination agreement
  between an Originating Depository Financial Institution and an originator in
  the Same Day ACH network?

Responses:
  A. To bind the originator to the Nacha Operating Rules and authorize the ODFI
     to receive transactions on the originator's behalf
  B. To establish the terms under which the ACH Operator will settle transactions
     between the ODFI and the Receiving Depository Financial Institution
  C. To designate a third-party service provider to perform compliance functions
     on behalf of the originator

Answer: A

Source citation:
  AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework, lines
  10067–10084. Also: FFIEC IT Examination Handbook: Retail Payment Systems
  Booklet, lines 2474–2483.

Q2 — Factual / JSQ
Sub-competency: Identify the required disclosures for each payment rail
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Knowing that Visa Direct originators must provide customers with
comprehensive Terms and Conditions before service use is a foundational
disclosure requirement a 2-year payments practitioner should recognize.

Stem:
  Which disclosure obligation do Visa Direct originators have toward customers
  before those customers begin using the Visa Direct service?

Responses:
  A. Originators must submit a copy of their privacy policy to Visa for
     compliance verification before providing it to customers
  B. Originators must furnish customers with comprehensive Terms and Conditions
     before the customer uses the Visa Direct service
  C. Originators must disclose daily reconciliation reports and a detailed
     pricing breakdown to customers at the time of each transaction

Answer: B

Source citation:
  AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework, lines
  10194–10203.

Q3 — Factual / SQ
Sub-competency: Compare warranties and indemnities by rail and rule set
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: The specific Mastercard Send liability cap — compensation received
from the customer OR USD 250,000, whichever is less — requires detailed
knowledge of network-specific indemnity terms beyond a 2-year practitioner's
working knowledge.

Stem:
  Under Mastercard Send standards, to what amount is Mastercard's total
  liability for damages to a customer capped?

Responses:
  A. The lesser of the total transaction value processed by the customer in the
     prior calendar year or USD 500,000
  B. The greater of the fees paid by the customer to Mastercard or USD 100,000,
     whichever provides greater protection to the customer
  C. The compensation received by Mastercard from the customer, or USD 250,000,
     whichever is less, unless prohibited by law

Answer: C

Source citation:
  AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework, "Warranties
  and Indemnities — Mastercard Send" section, lines 10258–10260.

----- PROCEDURAL QUESTIONS (2 JSQ + 1 SQ) -----

Q4 — Procedural / JSQ
Sub-competency: Compare warranties and indemnities by rail and rule set
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: The RFP Warranty breach process — submitting camt.056 with the
WNTB reason code within 95 calendar days — is an operational procedure that
FedNow practitioners encounter in warranty dispute workflows.

Stem:
  When a FedNow participant determines that a Request for Payment warranty was
  breached after the corresponding credit transfer has settled, what message
  must the Receiver FI send to seek a return of funds?

Responses:
  A. A Return Request (camt.056) message with the WNTB reason code, submitted
     within 95 calendar days of the credit transfer settlement date
  B. A Payment Status Report (pacs.002) message with the fraud reason code,
     submitted within 60 calendar days of the credit transfer settlement date
  C. A Payment Return (pacs.004) message with the warranty breach reason code,
     submitted within 30 calendar days of the credit transfer settlement date

Answer: A

Source citation:
  Federal Reserve Banks. FedNow Service Operating Procedures (June 24, 2025),
  lines 2073–2101. Also: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance
  Framework, lines 10238–10251.

Q5 — Procedural / JSQ
Sub-competency: Describe the roles of agreements among faster payments
stakeholders
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: Knowing that TCH's liability under the RTP Participant Agreement is
limited to its own gross negligence or intentional misconduct is working
knowledge for RTP practitioners who advise on indemnification risk.

Stem:
  Under the RTP Participant Agreement and Indemnity, for which of its own
  conduct is The Clearing House liable to participants?

Responses:
  A. Any losses arising from payment rejections caused by insufficient prefunded
     balances maintained by sending participants
  B. Its own gross negligence or intentional misconduct directly attributable
     to The Clearing House
  C. Fraud losses incurred by receiving participants when a sending participant
     fails to screen transactions before submission

Answer: B

Source citation:
  AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework, "Warranties
  and Indemnities — RTP Network" section, lines 10219–10228.

Q6 — Procedural / SQ
Sub-competency: Compare warranties and indemnities by rail and rule set
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: The rule that gateway operators assume the same warranties as ODFIs
— not RDFIs or ACH Operators — is a specific Nacha provision requiring deeper
network expertise to apply correctly.

Stem:
  Under the Nacha Operating Rules, which warranties does a gateway operator
  assume in the Same Day ACH network?

Responses:
  A. The same warranties as the Receiving Depository Financial Institution,
     including the obligation to indemnify the ODFI and ACH Operator for
     unauthorized return entries
  B. The same warranties as the Originating Depository Financial Institution,
     including the obligation to indemnify the RDFI and ACH Operator for
     warranty breaches
  C. The same warranties as the ACH Operator, including responsibility for
     settlement finality and the accuracy of transaction routing information

Answer: B

Source citation:
  AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework, "Warranties
  and Indemnities — Same Day ACH" section, lines 10215–10216.

----- CONDITIONAL QUESTIONS (1 JSQ + 2 SQ) -----

Q7 — Conditional / JSQ
Sub-competency: Describe the roles of disclosures among faster payments
stakeholders
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The FedNow participant indemnification condition — failure to
provide required disclosures and obtain necessary customer consents under
Operating Circular 8 — is a clear, single-rule condition a 2-year practitioner
should be able to apply.

Stem:
  Under what condition must a FedNow participant indemnify the Federal Reserve
  Banks against claims and losses?

Responses:
  A. The participant fails to fulfill its obligations under Federal Reserve
     Operating Circular 8, including providing required disclosures and
     obtaining necessary customer consents for Reserve Bank use of transaction
     records
  B. The participant's transaction volume exceeds the threshold established by
     the Federal Reserve Banks' fraud monitoring program in a given settlement
     period
  C. The participant uses a service provider whose security infrastructure does
     not meet the minimum standards outlined in the FedNow Service Operating
     Procedures

Answer: A

Source citation:
  AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework, "Required
  Disclosures — FedNow" section, lines 10167–10176.

---

Q8 — Conditional / SQ
Sub-competency: Compare warranties and indemnities by rail and rule set
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: Visa Direct's conditional liability for push-to-account OCTs — that
it applies only when directly attributable to Visa, and that Visa bears no
liability for downstream entities — requires synthesis of Visa's multi-layered
liability framework characteristic of a 5-year practitioner.

Stem:
  Under what condition does Visa assume liability for the loss or misdirection
  of funds in a Visa Direct push-to-account Original Credit Transaction?

Responses:
  A. When a downstream entity such as a partner bank or processing scheme causes
     the loss through actions taken outside the Visa Core Rules
  B. When the loss or misdirection of funds is directly attributable to Visa and
     falls within the limitations set out in the Visa Core Rules and Visa Product
     and Service Rules
  C. When the originator fails to provide the sender with required fee disclosures
     and the sender initiates a dispute within the timeframe permitted by
     applicable law

Answer: B

Source citation:
  AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework, "Warranties
  and Indemnities — Visa Direct" section, lines 10264–10270.

---

Q9 — Conditional / SQ
Sub-competency: Compare warranties and indemnities by rail and rule set
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The categorical prohibition on recovering special or consequential
damages from the Federal Reserve Banks — regardless of foreseeability or prior
notification — must be distinguished from the general direct-damage liability
standard, requiring deep knowledge of FedNow's liability framework.

Stem:
  Under what condition may a FedNow participant recover special or consequential
  damages from the Federal Reserve Banks arising from FedNow Service activity?

Responses:
  A. When the participant demonstrates that the Reserve Banks had prior knowledge
     of the potential for such damages and failed to exercise ordinary care in
     processing the relevant payment message
  B. When the loss results from the Reserve Banks' willful misconduct and the
     participant notified the Reserve Banks of the potential for consequential
     harm before the transaction was submitted
  C. Special or consequential damages may NOT be recovered from the Federal
     Reserve Banks under any condition, regardless of foreseeability or prior
     notification

Answer: C

Source citation:
  AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework, "Warranties
  and Indemnities — FedNow" section, lines 10241–10242.
