Plan — KSA 5002 (v2)
Laws and Regulations Applicable to Faster Payments Networks/Platforms
=========================================================================

KSA: 5000 – Governance Framework
  5002 – Laws and regulations applicable to faster payments
         networks/platforms
    – The interaction between private sector rules and other banking
      regulations
    – Identify the payment rails governed by laws and regulations
      applicable to faster payments
    – Identify the party responsible for governance of laws and
      regulations applicable to faster payments
    – Differentiate between consumer and non-consumer regulations
    – Compare and contrast private rule sets and federal regulation
    – Describe the objectives of the data privacy laws
    – Describe the obligations of faster payments stakeholders (by
      payment rail) under data privacy laws
    – Explain the application of the Bank Secrecy Act and
      Anti-Money Laundering regulations to faster payments
    – Explain the hierarchy of rules and regulations by payment rail

Question distribution: Factual (2 JSQ + 1 SQ) | Procedural (2 JSQ + 1 SQ)
                       Conditional (1 JSQ + 2 SQ)
Target answer distribution: A(3), B(3), C(3)

Note: v1 questions covered EFTA establishing the consumer EFT framework;
BSA primary purpose and AML program elements; UCC Article 4A/Reg J
Subparts B and C; ODFI OFAC screening in ACH; private/federal rule
interaction; Reg E personal/family/household purpose test; FedNow
hierarchy (Reg J and OC8 supersede Operating Procedures); and BSA
structuring-style suspicious activity. All nine v2 questions draw on
different passages and topics.

SOURCE MATERIAL SUMMARY

Sub-competency — Data privacy law objectives
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
AFPP Handbook, lines 9779–9788:
  Data privacy laws aim to protect consumers from fraud, scams, and
  identity theft. They safeguard sensitive information, prevent breaches
  of privacy and data security that could lead to identity theft,
  financial harm, and fraud. These laws establish rules requiring
  institutions to obtain consumer consent before sharing data,
  anonymize data, and set minimum standards on data storage and
  transmission across borders.

Sub-competency — Payment rails governed by specific laws (Table 60)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
AFPP Handbook, Table 60, lines 9692–9721:
  FedNow:          Regulation E, Regulation J Subparts B and C,
                   UCC Article 4A, FedNow Operating Rules
  RTP network:     Regulation E, UCC Article 4A, RTP Operating Rules,
                   RTP Participation Rules
  Same Day ACH:    Regulation E, UCC Article 4A, EFTA,
                   Nacha Operating Rules
  Mastercard Send: Regulation E, PCI DSS, Mastercard Operating Rules
  Visa Direct:     Regulation E, PCI DSS, Visa Operating Rules

  Key observation: Visa Direct and Mastercard Send are the only two
  U.S. faster payment rails that are governed by PCI DSS (in addition
  to Regulation E and their respective network rules). FedNow, RTP, and
  Same Day ACH are NOT subject to PCI DSS under Table 60.

Sub-competency — Data privacy obligations by payment rail
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
AFPP Handbook, lines 9799–9805 (FedNow):
  Compliance with Operating Circular 8 mandated. Participants using
  FedLine Solutions must maintain robust information security programs,
  including message signing using public/private key pairs, encryption
  of data in transit and at rest, and authentication protocols.

AFPP Handbook, lines 9808–9816 (RTP):
  Participants must maintain a robust information security program,
  restrict physical and logical access to customer data, ensure secure
  destruction of data, maintain detailed system architecture and
  intrusion detection specifications, undergo regular audits by
  independent auditors, conduct an annual review of security measure
  effectiveness, maintain data breach contingency plans, and perform
  thorough background checks on employees with access to customer
  information.

AFPP Handbook, lines 9819–9825 (Same Day ACH):
  Rigorous data security standards per Nacha Operating Rules; emphasis
  on protection throughout the data lifecycle; access controls and
  encryption for account information; self-assessments; identity
  verification of Third-Party Senders and Originators; compliance with
  state and federal laws on secure storage; alignment with regulatory
  requirements for "commercial reasonableness."

AFPP Handbook, lines 9836–9837 (Visa Direct):
  Two-Way SSL (Mutual SSL) authentication required; channel encryption
  required; username, password, and X509 security certificate issued
  by Visa; message-level encryption mandated.

Sub-competency — PCI DSS objectives and scope
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
AFPP Handbook, lines 8680–8684:
  PCI DSS was developed by Visa, Mastercard, and other payment card
  industry leaders to set a baseline level of protection for customers,
  merchants, and service providers; reduce fraud and the impact of data
  breaches; enhance consumer confidence; and protect the overall
  integrity of the payment system. It applies to all entities that
  store, process, or transmit cardholder data and/or sensitive
  authentication data.

PCIDSS Quick Reference Guide v3:
  PCI DSS represents technical and operational requirements designed to
  safeguard payment card account data and establish a secure cardholder
  data environment. Key requirements include protecting stored
  cardholder data, encrypting cardholder data in transit, restricting
  access by need-to-know, monitoring all access to cardholder data,
  maintaining an information security policy, and reviewing firewall
  configurations at least every six months.

Sub-competency — Regulation E consumer liability limits
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
12 CFR Part 1005 (Regulation E), Section 1005.6:
  Consumer liability for unauthorized electronic fund transfers is
  limited to $50 if the financial institution is notified within 2
  business days of discovering the unauthorized transfer. Liability
  increases to $500 if notice is given after 2 business days but
  within 60 calendar days of the statement date. The consumer may bear
  unlimited liability if notification is NOT provided within 60
  calendar days.

Sub-competency — Regulation J scope (three payment systems)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
12 CFR Part 210 (Regulation J), Sections 210.1, 210.25–210.26:
  Regulation J governs three distinct payment systems operated by the
  Federal Reserve: (1) Subpart A — collection of checks through Reserve
  Banks; (2) Subpart B — funds transfers through the Fedwire Funds
  Service; (3) Subpart C — funds transfers through the FedNow Service.
  Each subpart incorporates UCC Article 4A and establishes distinct
  rules for payment order execution, settlement, and participant
  obligations.

Sub-competency — OC 8 pre-participation security requirement
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Operating Circular 8 (April 1, 2026), Sections 3.0–8.0:
  Each FedNow participant must execute a FedNow Service Security
  Procedure Agreement with the Federal Reserve Bank before sending or
  receiving payment orders through the FedNow Service. OC 8, together
  with Regulation J and Operating Circular 5, forms the comprehensive
  governance framework for FedNow participants.

Sub-competency — Compare/contrast private rule sets and federal regulation
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
AFPP Handbook, lines 9756–9777:
  Federal regulations:
    – Enacted through structured process with public comment
    – Mandated standards as foundation of regulatory environment
    – May hinder timely adaptation to market demands and technology
  Private rule sets:
    – Immediate access to industry experts
    – Develop relevant interbank rules customized to evolving needs
    – Foster innovation and fair competition
    – Establish detailed loss-allocation policies among participants
    – Demonstrate speed of adaptation
  Synthesis: Synergy between the two creates a robust and balanced
  framework in which the strengths of each complement the limitations
  of the other.

  Key distinguishing characteristic of private rule sets: they
  establish detailed loss-allocation policies and customized operational
  standards that federal regulation does not prescribe, filling gaps
  left by the broader scope of federal law.

Sub-competency — RTP annual security review requirement
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
AFPP Handbook, lines 9808–9816:
  The RTP network requires participant information security programs to
  undergo an annual review of security measure effectiveness. This
  requirement applies to all participants regardless of transaction
  volume or breach history.

Q1 — Factual / JSQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: Data privacy law objectives
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Recognizing that data privacy laws protect consumers from
fraud, scams, and identity theft by establishing minimum standards on
data storage and transmission is foundational regulatory knowledge for
a 2-year faster payments practitioner.

Stem:
  What is the primary objective of data privacy laws as they apply to
  faster payments stakeholders?

Responses:
  A. To protect consumers from fraud, scams, and identity theft by
     safeguarding sensitive information and establishing minimum
     standards on data storage and transmission
  B. To establish interoperability standards that enable financial
     institutions to share consumer payment data across faster payment
     networks without requiring individual consent
  C. To require faster payment network operators to submit annual
     reports to federal regulators certifying compliance with consumer
     data security standards

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Data Privacy Laws" section, lines 9779–9788.

Q2 — Factual / JSQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: Identify payment rails governed by PCI DSS
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Knowing that Visa Direct and Mastercard Send — as card
network-based rails — are the U.S. faster payment rails subject to PCI
DSS is working knowledge for a 2-year practitioner managing compliance
across multiple payment rails.

Stem:
  According to the AFPP Handbook, which U.S. faster payment rails are
  governed by PCI DSS in addition to Regulation E and their respective
  network operating rules?

Responses:
  A. FedNow and the RTP network, because both process card-funded
     transactions as part of their standard credit transfer services
  B. Visa Direct and Mastercard Send, because both are card
     network-based payment rails that store, process, or transmit
     cardholder data
  C. Same Day ACH and FedNow, because both operate under Federal
     Reserve oversight, which requires cardholder data protection
     compliance

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
Table 60 – Applicable laws and regulations by payment rail, lines
9692–9721.

Q3 — Factual / SQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: Reg E consumer liability limits
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: The precise three-tier liability structure — $50, $500, and
unlimited — and the specific notification timeframes that trigger each
tier require detailed knowledge of Regulation E Section 1005.6 beyond
2-year working knowledge.

Stem:
  Under Regulation E, how does a consumer's liability for unauthorized
  electronic fund transfers change based on when the consumer notifies
  the financial institution?

Responses:
  A. Liability is capped at $50 regardless of when notification is
     provided, so long as the consumer submits a written dispute within
     60 calendar days of the statement date showing the unauthorized
     transfer
  B. Liability is capped at $50 if the consumer and financial
     institution have a pre-established error resolution agreement;
     otherwise the consumer bears full liability for unauthorized
     transfers
  C. Liability is limited to $50 if notification is provided within 2
     business days; increases to $500 if notification is provided after
     2 business days but within 60 calendar days; and may be unlimited
     if notification is NOT provided within 60 calendar days

Answer: C

Source: 12 CFR Part 1005 (Regulation E). Consumer Financial Protection
Bureau. Section 1005.6, pages 1–20.

Q4 — Procedural / JSQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: OC 8 pre-participation requirement for FedNow
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: Knowing that a financial institution must execute a FedNow
Service Security Procedure Agreement before sending or receiving payment
orders is a standard onboarding step that a 2-year practitioner
responsible for FedNow implementation should know.

Stem:
  According to Operating Circular 8, what must a financial institution
  complete before it is permitted to send or receive payment orders
  through the FedNow Service?

Responses:
  A. Execute a FedNow Service Security Procedure Agreement with the
     Federal Reserve Bank
  B. Submit a written certification to the Federal Reserve Bank
     confirming it has implemented a BSA/AML compliance program that
     includes FedNow-specific transaction monitoring
  C. Complete a pilot testing phase in the FedNow customer testing
     environment and receive written approval from the Federal Reserve
     Bank to begin live transaction processing

Answer: A

Source: Operating Circular 8 (April 1, 2026). Federal Reserve Banks.
FedNow Service. Sections 3.0–8.0, pages 1–20.

Q5 — Procedural / JSQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: Data privacy obligations — Visa Direct
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: Knowing the specific technical security measures Visa Direct
requires of its participants — Mutual SSL, channel encryption, and Visa-
issued X509 certificates — is working knowledge for a 2-year
practitioner managing Visa Direct compliance obligations.

Stem:
  Which data security measures does the Visa Direct network require of
  its participants to comply with network data privacy obligations?

Responses:
  A. Submission of annual third-party auditor reports certifying
     compliance with federal data security regulations applicable to
     card-present transactions
  B. Implementation of Two-Way SSL (Mutual SSL) authentication, channel
     encryption, and message-level encryption, supported by a username,
     password, and an X509 security certificate issued by Visa
  C. Maintenance of a written information security program with
     technical, physical, and administrative safeguards, including
     regular testing of safeguard effectiveness and proper disposal of
     personal data

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Data Privacy Obligations by Payment Rail" section, lines 9836–9837.

Q6 — Procedural / SQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: Reg J scope across three payment systems
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: The precise three-subpart structure of Regulation J —
assigning check collection to Subpart A, Fedwire to Subpart B, and
FedNow to Subpart C — is detailed regulatory architecture that requires
5+ years of expertise in Federal Reserve payment governance to apply
reliably.

Stem:
  Which three payment systems does Regulation J govern, and how does it
  structure its coverage?

Responses:
  A. Regulation J governs the ACH network (under Subpart A), the
     FedNow Service (under Subpart B), and the RTP network (under
     Subpart C), applying UCC Article 4A principles to each
  B. Regulation J governs check collection through Reserve Banks (under
     Subpart A), Fedwire Funds Service transfers (under Subpart B), and
     FedNow Service transfers (under Subpart C)
  C. Regulation J governs consumer electronic fund transfers (under
     Subpart A), business-to-business wire transfers (under Subpart B),
     and instant payment transactions (under Subpart C), regardless of
     which Federal Reserve system processes them

Answer: B

Source: 12 CFR Part 210 (Regulation J). Federal Reserve System.
Sections 210.1, 210.25–210.26, pages 1–40.

Q7 — Conditional / JSQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: When PCI DSS applies to a faster payments participant
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The clear, single-rule condition — that PCI DSS applies to
any entity that stores, processes, or transmits cardholder data —
is a foundational compliance threshold a 2-year practitioner working
with card-based payment rails should be able to apply.

Stem:
  Under what condition is a faster payment participant required to
  comply with PCI DSS requirements?

Responses:
  A. When the participant stores, processes, or transmits cardholder
     data as part of its faster payment operations
  B. When the participant processes more than the annual transaction
     volume threshold established by the card networks for their
     respective compliance tiers
  C. When the participant connects to a card network-based faster
     payment rail and has NOT completed the required annual self-
     assessment questionnaire within the preceding 12 months

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
lines 8680–8684. Also: PCIDSS Quick Reference Guide v3. PCI Security
Standards Council. Introduction and Overview.

Q8 — Conditional / SQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: When private rule sets impose obligations beyond federal
               law
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: Identifying the specific condition under which private rule
sets go beyond federal regulation — by establishing detailed loss-
allocation policies and customized operational standards that federal
law does not prescribe — requires synthesis of the comparative
governance analysis characteristic of a 5-year practitioner.

Stem:
  Under what condition do private sector payment rules impose
  obligations on financial institutions that go beyond the requirements
  of federal regulation, according to the AFPP Handbook?

Responses:
  A. When the federal regulation governing the payment rail was enacted
     before the rail was established and has not been updated to address
     rail-specific operational requirements
  B. When private rule sets establish detailed loss-allocation policies
     and customized operational standards that federal regulation does
     not prescribe, filling gaps left by the broader scope of federal
     law
  C. When the payment rail is operated by a non-governmental entity and
     participants have contractually agreed to be bound by the
     network's operating rules, superseding applicable federal
     requirements

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Comparison of Private Rule Sets and Federal Regulation," lines
9756–9777.

Q9 — Conditional / SQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: RTP annual security review — trigger condition
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: Understanding that the RTP network's annual security review
requirement applies universally to all participants — not only those
exceeding a volume threshold or those that have experienced a breach —
requires nuanced knowledge of RTP data privacy obligations beyond 2-year
working knowledge.

Stem:
  Under what condition does the RTP network require a participant's
  information security measures to undergo an annual review?

Responses:
  A. When the participant has experienced a confirmed data breach during
     the preceding calendar year and must certify to The Clearing House
     that corrective actions have been implemented
  B. When the participant exceeds a transaction volume threshold
     established by The Clearing House that triggers enhanced oversight
     and independent security auditing requirements
  C. When the participant maintains an information security program
     covering its RTP activities, because the RTP network requires an
     annual review of security measure effectiveness regardless of
     transaction volume or breach history

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Data Privacy Obligations by Payment Rail — TCH RTP" section, lines
9808–9816.
