Plan — KSA 5003 (v2)
Error Resolution Protections, Rights, and Liabilities of Payers and Payees
=========================================================================

KSA: 5000 – Governance Framework
  5003 – Error resolution protections, rights, and liabilities of
         payers and payees
    – Explain the liability of a payer for a misdirected payment
    – Differentiate the applicability of error resolution protections
      under Regulation E and UCC Article 4A
    – Explain the liabilities and error resolution responsibilities of
      the sending and receiving financial institutions
    – Define an error on a faster payment

Question distribution: Factual (2 JSQ + 1 SQ) | Procedural (2 JSQ + 1 SQ)
                       Conditional (1 JSQ + 2 SQ)
Target answer distribution: A(3), B(3), C(3)

FACTUAL QUESTIONS
=========================================================================

Q1 — Factual / JSQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Knowing that Regulation E Section 1005.11 defines errors to
include the omission of an electronic fund transfer from a periodic
statement — not just unauthorized transfers or wrong amounts — is
working knowledge for a 2-year practitioner responsible for consumer
error resolution.

According to Regulation E, which of the following is considered an
error subject to the error resolution procedures of Section 1005.11?

  A. The omission of an electronic fund transfer from a consumer's
     periodic statement
  B. A payment that exceeds the sending financial institution's
     internally configured transaction limit for faster payments
  C. The failure of a payment to settle by the next business day due
     to a system outage at the receiving financial institution

Answer: A

Source: 12 CFR Part 1005 (Regulation E). Consumer Financial Protection
Bureau. Section 1005.11(a)(1), page 14.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q2 — Factual / JSQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Understanding that a sending financial institution bears no
liability when it submits a Request for Return of Funds on the RTP
network — because it does not constitute cancellation of the payment —
is foundational knowledge for a 2-year practitioner handling RTP error
resolution.

When a sending financial institution submits a Request for Return of
Funds on the RTP network, what liability does the sending financial
institution bear to the receiving participant?

  A. The sending financial institution is liable for all costs incurred
     by the receiving participant in investigating the return request,
     including any funds that cannot be recovered from the end customer
  B. The sending financial institution bears no liability to the
     receiving participant, because a Request for Return of Funds does
     NOT constitute the cancellation of an RTP payment
  C. The sending financial institution is liable for returning the full
     amount of the original payment to the receiving participant if the
     return request is denied or the funds cannot be retrieved from the
     end customer

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
lines 6302–6310.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q3 — Factual / SQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Knowing the specific conditions under which a Visa Direct
originating credit transaction may be reversed — incorrect credentials,
incorrect amount, duplicate processing, or incorrect transaction code
— requires detailed knowledge of Visa Direct error resolution
procedures beyond 2-year working knowledge.

According to the AFPP Handbook, which of the following is a valid
condition for initiating a reversal of an originating credit
transaction on the Visa Direct network?

  A. The receiving financial institution submitted a return request
     within the applicable network timeframe and the original
     transaction settled more than 24 hours prior to the reversal
     request
  B. The recipient declined to accept the payment and the sending
     financial institution initiated a recall within the applicable
     reversal window permitted under Visa operating rules
  C. The original transaction involved incorrect payment credentials,
     an incorrect transaction amount, duplicate processing, or an
     incorrect transaction code

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
lines 6328–6332.

Q4 — Procedural / JSQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: Knowing that an ODFI corrects an erroneous or unauthorized
Same Day ACH entry by sending a reversing entry to the RDFI is a
standard operational procedure a 2-year practitioner handling ACH
error corrections should know.

How does an ODFI in Same Day ACH correct an entry that was
transmitted erroneously or without the originator's proper
authorization?

  A. By sending a reversing entry to the RDFI to address the incorrect
     or unauthorized entry
  B. By submitting a return request directly to the ACH Operator
     within 24 hours of the settlement date on which the erroneous
     entry processed
  C. By notifying the receiving financial institution through a
     standard Request for Information message and requesting that the
     funds be placed on hold pending investigation

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
lines 6313–6317.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q5 — Procedural / JSQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: Knowing that a financial institution must correct a confirmed
Regulation E error within one business day of making that determination
is a standard compliance procedure a 2-year practitioner in a consumer
payments compliance role should know.

Under Regulation E, what must a financial institution do within one
business day after determining that a consumer's reported error
actually occurred?

  A. Notify the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau of the error
     determination and provide documentation of the correction steps
     taken
  B. Correct the error and credit the consumer's account for the
     amount of the error, including applicable interest where the
     account is interest-bearing
  C. Provide the consumer with a written explanation of the findings
     and the documents relied upon in making the error determination

Answer: B

Source: 12 CFR Part 1005 (Regulation E). Consumer Financial Protection
Bureau. Section 1005.11(c), page 15.

Q6 — Procedural / SQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: The specific initial response deadline — midnight Eastern
Time of the next standard business day — and the required ISO 20022
message type (camt.029) for return request responses requires expert
knowledge of FedNow exception handling procedures beyond 2-year
working knowledge.

When a FedNow participant receives a return request (camt.056), by
when must it provide its initial response — whether approving,
denying, or indicating the request is under investigation?

  A. Within 20 business days of receiving the return request,
     consistent with the timeframe applicable to Request for Payment
     warranty breach claims on the FedNow Service
  B. Within 3 business days of receiving the return request, using
     the pacs.004 message to confirm acceptance or the pacs.008
     message to confirm denial
  C. No later than midnight Eastern Time of the next standard business
     day, using the camt.029 message with an appropriate response
     code for approval, denial, or ongoing investigation

Answer: C

Source: FedNow Service Operating Procedures (June 2025). Federal
Reserve Banks. Section on Payment Returns, lines 4207–4261.

Q7 — Conditional / JSQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The clear, single-rule condition — that the RDFI has
discretion to decline an ODFI's reversing entry for valid causes such
as insufficient funds — is a foundational Same Day ACH error resolution
principle a 2-year practitioner should be able to apply.

When an ODFI sends a reversing entry to an RDFI in Same Day ACH,
under what condition may the RDFI decline to return the entry?

  A. When the RDFI determines, at its own discretion, that there are
     valid reasons to decline the return — such as insufficient funds
     in the receiving account or other valid causes
  B. When the original entry was a credit to a consumer account and
     the consumer has already received and spent the funds, making the
     reversal technically impractical under the Nacha Operating Rules
  C. When the ODFI submitted the reversing entry more than one
     business day after the original settlement date, placing the
     transaction outside the RDFI's standard return window

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
lines 6318–6320.

Q8 — Conditional / SQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: Identifying the specific condition that triggers the 10-
business-day final response deadline — the PDCR investigating response
code — requires synthesis of FedNow return request message flow and
multi-step response codes, characteristic of a 5-year practitioner.

Under what condition must a FedNow participant provide its final
response to a return request within 10 business days, rather than
by midnight Eastern Time of the next standard business day?

  A. When the return request was submitted by the Sender financial
     institution more than 30 calendar days after the original credit
     transfer settled, triggering the extended investigation timeline
     under FedNow Service operating procedures
  B. When the Receiver financial institution responds to the camt.056
     with a PDCR response code, indicating that the return request
     requires additional investigation before a final determination
     can be made
  C. When the return request involves an amount that exceeds the
     FedNow transaction limit and the Receiver financial institution
     must obtain regulatory approval before approving or denying the
     fund return

Answer: B

Source: FedNow Service Operating Procedures (June 2025). Federal
Reserve Banks. Section on Payment Returns, lines 4207–4261.

Q9 — Conditional / SQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: Distinguishing the 95-calendar-day RfP warranty breach claim
window from the 60-calendar-day standard return request window — and
knowing how the Sender financial institution's 20-business-day response
obligation interacts with it — requires multi-concept synthesis
characteristic of a 5-year practitioner.

Under what condition must a Receiver financial institution on the
FedNow Service submit its return request when claiming a breach of
the RfP Warranty?

  A. Within 60 calendar days of the credit transfer settlement date,
     the same timeframe applicable to standard payment return requests
     on the FedNow Service
  B. Within 20 business days of determining that the RfP Warranty was
     breached, the same timeframe within which the Sender financial
     institution must respond to the claim
  C. Within 95 calendar days of the date the credit transfer
     associated with the Request for Payment settled, as established
     by FedNow Service procedures for RfP warranty breach claims

Answer: C

Source: FedNow Service Operating Procedures (June 2025). Federal
Reserve Banks. Section on Request for Payment Warranty Claims, lines
2051–2170.
