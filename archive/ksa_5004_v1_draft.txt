Q1 — Factual / JSQ

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: The purpose of an origination agreement in Same Day ACH — binding
the originator to the Nacha Operating Rules and authorizing the ODFI to
receive transactions on the originator's behalf — is standard working
knowledge for a 2-year ACH practitioner.

Which of the following describes a primary purpose of an origination
agreement between an Originating Depository Financial Institution and an
originator in the Same Day ACH network?

  A. To bind the originator to the Nacha Operating Rules and authorize the
     Originating Depository Financial Institution to receive transactions on
     the originator's behalf
  B. To establish the terms under which the ACH Operator will settle
     transactions between the Originating and Receiving Depository Financial
     Institutions
  C. To designate a third-party service provider to perform compliance
     functions on behalf of the originator

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Required Agreements — Same Day ACH" section, lines 10067–10084. Also:
FFIEC IT Examination Handbook: Retail Payment Systems Booklet,
"Third-Party Sender Requirements" section, lines 2474–2483.

Q2 — Factual / JSQ

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Knowing that Visa Direct originators must furnish customers with
comprehensive Terms and Conditions before service use is a foundational
disclosure requirement a 2-year payments practitioner should recognize.

Which disclosure obligation do Visa Direct originators have toward
customers before those customers begin using the Visa Direct service?

  A. Originators must submit a copy of their privacy policy to Visa for
     compliance verification before providing it to customers
  B. Originators must furnish customers with comprehensive Terms and
     Conditions before the customer uses the Visa Direct service
  C. Originators must disclose daily reconciliation reports and a detailed
     pricing breakdown to customers at the time of each transaction

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Required Disclosures — Visa Direct" section, lines 10194–10203.

Q3 — Factual / SQ

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: The specific Mastercard Send liability cap — compensation
received from the customer OR USD 250,000, whichever is less — requires
detailed knowledge of network-specific indemnity terms beyond a 2-year
practitioner's working knowledge.

Under Mastercard Send standards, to what amount is Mastercard's total
liability for damages to a customer capped?

  A. The lesser of the total transaction value processed by the customer in
     the prior calendar year or USD 500,000
  B. The greater of the fees paid by the customer to Mastercard or USD
     100,000, whichever provides greater protection to the customer
  C. The compensation received by Mastercard from the customer, or USD
     250,000, whichever is less, unless prohibited by law

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Warranties and Indemnities — Mastercard Send" section, lines 10258–10260.

Q4 — Procedural / JSQ

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: The Request for Payment warranty breach procedure — submitting a
camt.056 with the WNTB reason code within 95 calendar days — is an
operational workflow that FedNow practitioners encounter in warranty
dispute processing.

When a FedNow participant determines that a Request for Payment warranty
was breached after the corresponding credit transfer has settled, what
message must the Receiver FI send to seek a return of funds?

  A. A Return Request (camt.056) message with the WNTB reason code,
     submitted within 95 calendar days of the credit transfer settlement
     date
  B. A Payment Status Report (pacs.002) message with the fraud reason code,
     submitted within 60 calendar days of the credit transfer settlement
     date
  C. A Payment Return (pacs.004) message with the warranty breach reason
     code, submitted within 30 calendar days of the credit transfer
     settlement date

Answer: A

Source: Federal Reserve Banks. FedNow Service Operating Procedures
(June 24, 2025), "Request for Payment Warranty" section, lines 2073–2101.
Also: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Warranties and Indemnities — FedNow" section, lines 10238–10251.

Q5 — Procedural / JSQ

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: Knowing that The Clearing House's liability under the RTP
Participant Agreement is limited to its own gross negligence or intentional
misconduct is working knowledge for RTP practitioners who advise on
indemnification risk.

Under the RTP Participant Agreement and Indemnity, for which of its own
conduct is The Clearing House liable to participants?

  A. Any losses arising from payment rejections caused by insufficient
     prefunded balances maintained by sending participants
  B. Its own gross negligence or intentional misconduct directly
     attributable to The Clearing House
  C. Fraud losses incurred by receiving participants when a sending
     participant fails to screen transactions before submission

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Warranties and Indemnities — RTP Network" section, lines 10219–10228.

Q7 — Conditional / JSQ

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The FedNow participant indemnification trigger — failure to
fulfill obligations under Operating Circular 8, including providing
required disclosures and obtaining necessary customer consents — is a
clear, single-rule condition a 2-year practitioner should be able to apply.

Under what condition must a FedNow participant indemnify the Federal
Reserve Banks against claims and losses?

  A. The participant fails to fulfill its obligations under Federal Reserve
     Operating Circular 8, including providing required disclosures and
     obtaining necessary customer consents for Reserve Bank use of
     transaction records
  B. The participant's transaction volume exceeds the threshold established
     by the Federal Reserve Banks' fraud monitoring program in a given
     settlement period
  C. The participant uses a service provider whose security infrastructure
     does NOT meet the minimum standards outlined in the FedNow Service
     Operating Procedures

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Required Disclosures — FedNow" section, lines 10167–10176.

Q8 — Conditional / SQ

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: Visa Direct's conditional liability for push-to-account Original
Credit Transactions — applying only when loss is directly attributable to
Visa and excluding downstream entities — requires synthesis of Visa's
multi-layered liability framework characteristic of a 5-year practitioner.

Under what condition does Visa assume liability for the loss or
misdirection of funds in a Visa Direct push-to-account Original Credit
Transaction?

  A. When a downstream entity such as a partner financial institution or
     processing scheme causes the loss through actions taken outside the
     Visa Core Rules
  B. When the loss or misdirection of funds is directly attributable to
     Visa and falls within the limitations set out in the Visa Core Rules
     and Visa Product and Service Rules
  C. When the originator fails to provide the sender with required fee
     disclosures and the sender initiates a dispute within the timeframe
     permitted by applicable law

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Warranties and Indemnities — Visa Direct" section, lines 10264–10270.

Q9 — Conditional / SQ

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The categorical prohibition on recovering special or
consequential damages from the Federal Reserve Banks — regardless of
foreseeability or prior notification — must be distinguished from the
general direct-damage liability standard, requiring deep knowledge of
FedNow's liability framework.

Under what condition may a FedNow participant recover special or
consequential damages from the Federal Reserve Banks arising from FedNow
Service activity?

  A. When the participant demonstrates that the Reserve Banks had prior
     knowledge of the potential for such damages and failed to exercise
     ordinary care in processing the relevant payment message
  B. When the loss results from the Reserve Banks' willful misconduct and
     the participant notified the Reserve Banks of the potential for
     consequential harm before the transaction was submitted
  C. Special or consequential damages may NOT be recovered from the Federal
     Reserve Banks under any condition, regardless of foreseeability or
     prior notification

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Warranties and Indemnities — FedNow" section, lines 10241–10242.

     breaches
