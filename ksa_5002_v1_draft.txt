Draft Test Questions

Q1 — Factual

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Knowing that the EFTA established the consumer EFT framework is
foundational regulatory knowledge expected of any 2-year payments practitioner.

Which federal law establishes the basic framework of rights, liabilities, and
responsibilities of participants in consumer electronic fund transfer systems?

A. The Electronic Fund Transfer Act
B. The Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act
C. UCC Article 4A

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
lines 9308–9312. Also: FFIEC IT Examination Handbook: Retail Payment Systems
Booklet, lines 521–525.

Q2 — Factual

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: The BSA's primary purpose and core obligations are standard
regulatory knowledge for a 2-year payments practitioner working in any
compliance-adjacent role.

What is the primary purpose of the Bank Secrecy Act as it applies to financial
institutions participating in faster payment networks?

A. To establish consumer liability limits for unauthorized electronic fund
   transfers processed through faster payment rails
B. To impose anti-money laundering program, record keeping, and suspicious
   activity reporting requirements on all financial institutions
C. To require financial institutions to maintain data privacy safeguards for
   customer information collected during payment processing

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Application of the Bank Secrecy Act and Anti-Money Laundering regulations to
Faster Payments," lines 9845–9852. Also: FFIEC IT Examination Handbook: Retail
Payment Systems Booklet, lines 1947–1956.

Q3 — Factual

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Knowing that the Federal Reserve incorporated UCC Article 4A
specifically through Regulation J Subparts B and C — rather than through the
FedNow Operating Procedures or a separate statute — requires regulatory depth
beyond working practitioner knowledge.

How did the Federal Reserve incorporate UCC Article 4A rules to govern FedNow
transactions?

A. By enacting the Electronic Fund Transfer Act to extend UCC Article 4A rules
   to consumer faster payment transactions processed through FedNow
B. By requiring FedNow participants to adopt UCC Article 4A directly through
   their FedNow Service participation agreements
C. By incorporating UCC Article 4A rules into Regulation J Subparts B and C

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
lines 9664–9666. Also: Federal Reserve Banks. FedNow Service Operating
Procedures (June 24, 2025), lines 333–334.

Q4 — Procedural

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: ODFI responsibility for OFAC screening in domestic ACH transactions
is a standard compliance procedure that a 2-year ACH practitioner applies
in day-to-day work.

In a domestic ACH transaction, which party is responsible for verifying that
the originator of the ACH instruction is not a blocked party under OFAC
regulations?

A. The Originating Depository Financial Institution
B. The ACH Operator
C. The Receiving Depository Financial Institution

Answer: A

Source: FFIEC IT Examination Handbook: Retail Payment Systems Booklet, "Office
of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC)" section, lines 1961–1984.

A. Customer credit scoring, transaction velocity limits, and automated payment

Q6 — Procedural

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Application
Rationale: Distinguishing the complementary — rather than purely hierarchical —
relationship between federal regulatory frameworks and private sector rules
requires synthesis across governance concepts that a 2-year practitioner
may oversimplify.

How do private sector payment rules and federal banking regulations function
in relation to each other within the faster payments governance framework?

A. Federal regulations supersede private sector rules in all cases, with private
   rules applying only when federal law does not address a specific payment
   scenario
B. Private sector rules supersede federal regulations for transactions between
   financial institutions, while federal regulations govern only consumer-facing
   payment activities
C. Federal regulations establish legal frameworks that guide the private sector,
   while private sector rules develop operational practices that support payment
   innovation within those frameworks

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
lines 9606–9612. Also: FFIEC IT Examination Handbook: Retail Payment Systems
Booklet, lines 1941–1947.

Q7 — Conditional

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The personal/family/household account purpose test is the
foundational trigger for consumer protection under Regulation E — a 2-year
practitioner should recognize and apply this condition without specialist
expertise.

Under what condition does Regulation E apply to an electronic fund transfer
rather than UCC Article 4A?

A. The transfer involves an account held for personal, family, or household
   purposes
B. The transfer is initiated through a faster payment network that has
   incorporated federal consumer protection standards into its operating rules
C. The transfer amount exceeds the transaction limit established by the payment
   network's operating rules

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Comparison between consumer and non-consumer regulations," lines 9734–9749.
Also: FFIEC IT Examination Handbook: Retail Payment Systems Booklet,
lines 521–525.

Q8 — Conditional

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: Knowing the specific precedence order within FedNow governance and
applying it to a conflict scenario requires regulatory depth characteristic of
a 5-year practitioner advising on governance risk.

When a FedNow participant's obligations under the FedNow Service Operating
Procedures conflict with the requirements of Regulation J or Federal Reserve
Operating Circular 8, which authority governs?

A. The FedNow Service Operating Procedures govern because they represent the
   most current and operationally specific guidance issued by the Federal
   Reserve Banks
B. Regulation J and Operating Circular 8 govern because they supersede the
   FedNow Service Operating Procedures to the extent of any inconsistency
C. The participant's internal compliance program governs because it must
   reconcile all applicable federal and network-level requirements

Answer: B

Source: Federal Reserve Banks. FedNow Service Operating Procedures
(June 24, 2025), lines 333–334. Also: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5
– Governance Framework, Table 60, lines 9692–9721.

Q9 — Conditional

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: Identifying structuring-style behavior in a faster payments context
requires synthesis of BSA/AML monitoring principles with faster-payment-specific
risk factors, characteristic of a 5-year practitioner.

Under what condition should a financial institution's faster payments monitoring
program flag account activity as suspicious for potential money laundering?

A. The account holder initiates a Request for Payment message without a prior
   business relationship with the payment receiver
B. The account's transaction volume exceeds the maximum transaction limit
   established by the faster payment network's operating rules
C. A recently opened account makes frequent payments in amounts just under
   reporting thresholds or transaction limits

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 5 – Governance Framework,
"Application of the Bank Secrecy Act and Anti-Money Laundering regulations to
Faster Payments," lines 9858–9868.
