SECTION A: FedNow Participation Types
  Source: AFPP Handbook, lines 3018–3058, 3542–3560

  Participation types (customer credit transfers and RfPs):
  - Receive credit transfers (receive only): cannot initiate payments; can
    return payments (pacs.004). CAN send RfPs (pain.013) but NOT eligible
    to receive RfPs because they cannot initiate customer credit transfers
    as a response. (lines 3031–3034)
  - Send and receive credit transfers: can send and receive payments
    (pacs.008) and return messages (pacs.004); can send RfPs; may choose
    to receive RfPs. (lines 3038–3039, 3553–3555)
  - Receive Requests-for-Payment (RfPs): can receive RfPs (pain.013) and
    can send and receive payments (pacs.008). (lines 3043–3045, 3559–3560)
  - LMT Send and Receive: can send and receive FI credit transfers (pacs.009)
  - LMT Receive Only: can receive FI credit transfers (pacs.009)
  - Settlement Only: CANNOT send nor receive FI credit transfers (pacs.009)
    OR customer payment messages (pacs.008); can exchange non-value system
    messages (camt.060, camt.052, admi.002, etc.) (lines 3233–3236)

  Respondent vs. Correspondent:
  - Respondent FIs: access FedNow via correspondent that maintains a Master
    Account; correspondent settles on respondent's behalf. (lines 2094–2096)
  - Correspondent FIs: maintain Federal Reserve Master Account on behalf of
    respondent FIs; responsible for settlement and compliance. (lines 2100–2104)

SECTION B: RTP Participation Types
  Source: AFPP Handbook, lines 3059–3073
  Source: RTP Participation Rules (July 21, 2025), Section II, page 3

  Four defined categories:
  1. Funding Participant: party to RTP Prefunded Balance Account Agreement;
     requests/receives disbursements; prefunds for itself if a Sending
     Participant.
  2. Non-funding Participant: NOT a Funding Participant; has a Funding Agent
     designated to act on its behalf with respect to funding obligations.
  3. Receiving Participant: holds the Receiver's Account; receives a
     Payment Message.
  4. Sending Participant: holds the Sender's Account; initiates a Payment
     Message.

  Mandatory combination rule (line 3071–3072; RTP Rules p.3 Sec. II.B):
  Every participant MUST be (i) either a Funding or Non-funding Participant
  AND (ii) a Receiving Participant. A participant also MAY be a Sending
  Participant. This means every participant receives payments; sending is
  optional.

  Only depository institutions are eligible for RTP participation.
  (RTP Participation Rules, Section I.A, page 3)

  Third-Party Service Providers (RTP Participation Rules, Section III, pp. 4–5):
  - Participant may designate a TPSP to send/receive transmissions on its behalf.
  - TPSP must comply with all relevant provisions of RTP Participation Rules
    and RTP Operating Rules as if the TPSP were a Participant.
  - Participants are FULLY responsible and liable to TCH and other Participants
    for all acts or omissions of their TPSPs with respect to the RTP System.
    (Section III.D.1, page 4)

SECTION C: Same Day ACH Participation Types
  Source: AFPP Handbook, lines 3074–3081

  - ODFI: receives payment instructions from originators; forwards entries
    to the ACH Operator.
  - RDFI: receives ACH entries from the ACH Operator; posts to receiver's
    account.
  - An FI may participate as RDFI without being an ODFI; HOWEVER, if an FI
    chooses to originate ACH entries, it MUST also agree to act as an RDFI.
    (line 3080–3081)

SECTION D: Mastercard Send Participation Types
  Source: AFPP Handbook, lines 3082–3138

  - Three programs: (1) MoneySend Payment & Funding Transactions,
    (2) Other Network Transactions, (3) Gaming and Gambling Payments.
  - Originating Institution (OI): financial institution providing funds
    transfer services; must register for the relevant program(s).
  - Transaction Initiator (TI): partner entity that can be sponsored by an OI.
  - Receiving Institutions (issuers): do NOT need to register for the
    MoneySend Payment Transactions Program, but they MUST notify Mastercard
    when ready to accept those transactions and must adhere to program
    standards. (lines 3136–3138)

SECTION E: Visa Direct Participation Types
  Source: AFPP Handbook, lines 3139–3144
  Source: Visa Core Rules and Visa Product and Service Rules, Section 8.4,
          pages 591–594

  - Originators must be a licensed Visa acquirer OR be sponsored by a
    licensed Visa acquirer.
  - Third-party service providers who offer push payment services to end
    users can be sponsored by a Visa licensed acquirer to offer push payment
    capabilities to Visa card accounts.

  Visa Direct participant roles (Visa rules, Section 8.4):
  - Originating Acquirer: initiates OCTs; must validate sender data and
    comply with applicable AML laws and anti-terrorist financing standards
    before originating an OCT. (Section 8.4.1.2, p.592)
  - Recipient Issuer: receives the OCT; must post the OCT to the Payment
    Credential within 2 business days of receipt, OR dispute the OCT to the
    Originating Acquirer. (Section 8.4.2.2, p.594)
  - Fast Funds: if the Recipient Issuer participates in Fast Funds, it must
    make funds available to the cardholder within 30 minutes of approving
    the OCT. (Section 8.4.2.2, p.594)

  Source: AFPP Handbook, Table 22, lines 3564–3679

  Credit Transfer Receive Only:

  Credit Transfer Send/Receive:
    pacs.008 — SEND: Mandatory  / RECEIVE: Mandatory
    pacs.002 — SEND: Mandatory  / RECEIVE: Mandatory
    camt.056 — SEND: Mandatory  / RECEIVE: Mandatory
    pacs.004 — SEND: Mandatory  / RECEIVE: Mandatory
    pain.013 — SEND: Optional   / RECEIVE: Prohibited
    pain.014 — SEND: N/A        / RECEIVE: Conditional

  Send/Receive with Receive RfP:
    pacs.008 — SEND: Mandatory  / RECEIVE: Mandatory
    pacs.002 — SEND: Mandatory  / RECEIVE: Mandatory
    camt.056 — SEND: Optional   / RECEIVE: Mandatory
    pacs.004 — SEND: Mandatory  / RECEIVE: Mandatory

    pain.014 — SEND: Mandatory  / RECEIVE: Conditional

  KEY DISTINCTION (lines 3542–3549):
  Receive-only participant: NOT able to send pacs.008; CAN send pain.013

SECTION G: Message Types by Participant Type — RTP
  Source: AFPP Handbook, Table 23, lines 3689–3728

  Receiving-Only Participant:
    pacs.008 — SEND: Prohibited / RECEIVE: Mandatory
    pacs.002 — SEND: Mandatory  / RECEIVE: Mandatory

  Sending and Receiving Participant:
    pacs.008 — SEND: Mandatory  / RECEIVE: Mandatory
    pacs.002 — SEND: Mandatory  / RECEIVE: Mandatory

  Additional system messages (line 3727–3729):
    admn.001: sign-on message
    admn.003: sign-off message
    admn.006: echo response message

SECTION H: FedNow Planned Downtime Sign-Off Requirement
  Source: FedNow Service Operating Procedures, lines 426–428

  "For planned downtime, Participants that are enabled to receive instant
  payments (Customer Credit Transfer Send and Receive or Receive Only
  participation types) are required to sign off via a Participant broadcast
  message (admi.004) or through the FedNow interface, if the downtime
  affects its ability to respond to pacs.008 and pacs.004 messages within
  the reserved response time or affect its ability to immediately make the
  funds available."

for a 2-year FedNow practitioner.

  Which action can a FedNow receive-only participant perform even though it
  cannot initiate customer credit transfers?

Responses:
  A. Send a Request for Payment to a financial institution that can initiate
     a credit transfer in response

     the receive-only participant to fund a payment
  C. Initiate a Liquidity Management Transfer to prefund its settlement
     account at the Federal Reserve

Q3 — Factual / SQ
Sub-competency: Describe the different types of participation by rail
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Knowing that a FedNow Settlement-Only participant is prohibited
from sending or receiving both pacs.009 (FI credit transfers) and pacs.008
(customer credit transfers) — while still being able to exchange system
messages — requires deep knowledge of FedNow's participation type matrix
beyond 2-year working knowledge.

  Which message types are prohibited for a FedNow Settlement-Only

  A. pacs.009 (Financial Institution Credit Transfer) and pacs.002 (Payment

  C. pacs.009 (Financial Institution Credit Transfer) and pacs.008 (Customer
     Credit Transfer)

Answer: C

  AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 1 – Fundamentals of Faster Payments,
  "Liquidity management" section, lines 3233–3236.

Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Application

  B. pacs.002 (Payment Status Report), which Receiving-Only participants

  C. pain.013 (Request for Payment), which is required for all participant

Rationale: The specific rule that a FedNow Credit Transfer Receive-Only

pain.013 — because receiving an RfP requires the ability to respond with a
credit transfer — requires deep knowledge of the FedNow message permission
matrix beyond a 2-year practitioner's working knowledge.

  Which describes a FedNow Credit Transfer Receive-Only participant's
  permissions for the pain.013 (Request for Payment) message?

  A. The participant is prohibited from both sending and receiving pain.013

  B. The participant must receive pain.013 messages as a mandatory
     requirement but is prohibited from sending them
  C. The participant may optionally send pain.013 messages but is prohibited
     from receiving them

Source:
  AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 1 – Fundamentals of Faster Payments,
  Table 22 – Message Types by Participant – FedNow, lines 3649–3658.
  Also: lines 3542–3549.

Q7 — Conditional / JSQ
Sub-competency: Describe the different types of participation by rail
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The rule that a financial institution choosing to originate ACH
entries must also agree to act as an RDFI is a clear, single-rule condition
under the Nacha Operating Rules that a 2-year ACH practitioner should know.

Stem:
  Under what condition must a financial institution that originates ACH
  entries also agree to act as a Receiving Depository Financial Institution
  in the Same Day ACH network?

Responses:
  A. Whenever the financial institution chooses to originate ACH entries,
     regardless of entry type or origination volume
  B. When the financial institution's total origination volume exceeds the
     threshold established by the ACH Operator for the current processing
     window
  C. When the financial institution uses a third-party service provider to
     forward ACH entries to the ACH Operator on its behalf

Answer: A

Source:
  AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 1 – Fundamentals of Faster Payments,
  "Same Day ACH participant types" section, lines 3080–3081.

Q8 — Conditional / SQ
Sub-competency: Describe the different types of participation by rail
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The rule that an RTP Participant is fully responsible to TCH and
other Participants for all acts and omissions of its Third-Party Service
Provider — not just for out-of-scope actions — requires knowledge of the
specific TCH RTP Participation Rules liability framework characteristic of
a 5-year practitioner.

Stem:
  Under what condition is an RTP Participant liable to The Clearing House
  and other Participants for actions taken by its Third-Party Service
  Provider in connection with the RTP System?

Responses:
  A. Only when the Third-Party Service Provider takes actions outside the
     scope of the services described in the Participant's agreement with
     The Clearing House
  B. At all times, for any act or omission of the Third-Party Service
     Provider with respect to the RTP System and the Participant's
     obligations under the RTP Participation Rules and RTP Operating Rules
  C. Only when The Clearing House has formally determined that the
     Third-Party Service Provider is no longer eligible to provide
     services to RTP Participants

Answer: B

Source:
  RTP Participation Rules (The Clearing House, July 21, 2025), Section III
  – Third-Party Service Providers, Part D.1 – Participant Liability for
  Third-Party Service Providers, page 4.

Q9 — Conditional / SQ
Sub-competency: Message types required by participation type
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The specific FedNow obligation that triggers a Receive-Only
participant's sign-off requirement — planned downtime that affects its
ability to respond to pacs.008 and pacs.004 within the reserved response
time — requires synthesis of participation type obligations and message
type requirements in the FedNow Operating Procedures.

Stem:
  Under what condition must a FedNow Credit Transfer Receive-Only
  participant sign off from the FedNow Service using a participant broadcast
  message (admi.004)?

Responses:
  A. When the participant transitions from a Receive-Only participation type
     to a Send and Receive participation type and requires a new connection
     profile
  B. When the participant's Federal Reserve Master Account balance falls
     below the minimum threshold established by the Federal Reserve Bank
     for intraday overdraft capacity
  C. When planned downtime affects the participant's ability to respond to
     pacs.008 and pacs.004 messages within the reserved response time or to
     immediately make funds available to customers

Answer: C

Source:
  Federal Reserve Banks. FedNow Service Operating Procedures (June 24,
  2025), "Planned Downtime" section, lines 426–428.
