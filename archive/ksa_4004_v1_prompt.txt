Refining and Improving Items

    Pose a complete, unambiguous question.
        Use a complete question format and avoid the incomplete sentence format. Most of the text you write should
    appear in the stem (not the response options).
        Do NOT try to trick the test taker with complex or confusing clauses and sentence structures.
        Avoid the use of negatively phrased questions unless absolutely necessary.
        Fill-in-the-blank and True/False questions are NOT allowed.

        A good example: 
            Which participant is involved in the Same Day ACH process flow?
                 Sending Participant
                 Operator
                 Receiving FI
        
        A bad example:
            Sender, Sending Participant, and _____ are NOT:
                 Receiving Participant; involved in the Same Day ACH process flow.
                 Originator; required to execute agreements with each RDFI.
                 Operator; owners of settlement accounts at the Federal Reserve.

    Make sure the stem is easy to read and understand.
        Use active voice.
        Write from the 3rd person perspective.
            Use generic position titles (e.g., risk manager, board of directors, leadership); do NOT use “he” or “she”.

        A good example: 
          The key advantage of the FraudClassifier℠ model is the ability to classify fraud independently of payment type, characteristics, and:
             channel.
             product.
             sender.

        A bad example: 
          Focusing on a series of questions, gaining a holistic fraudulent events view sophisticatedly applied across the industry, the FraudClassifier model has key advantages. Which of the following do you think is a key advantage of the model?
             Independent fraud classification by payment type,characteristics, and channel
             Independent fraud classification by payment type, characteristics, and product
             Independent fraud classification by payment type, characteristics, and sender

    Make sure the responses are properly structured.
        Write responses that are logical answers to the specific question you’re asking. Do NOT include responses that are silly, absurd, impractical, ridiculous, or fake.
        Make the responses equal in length and grammatical structure.
        Ensure there is only one correct response.
            Ensure they do not cover overlapping content.
            Vary the position of the correct response.

        A good example:
            When can a receiving financial institution use an Accept Without Posting response to an instant credit transfer sent to one of its customer accounts?
               The receiving financial institution's system requires more time to post funds in its core banking system.
               The identified recipient is not entitled or permitted to receive the payment for legal or compliance reasons.
               The sending or receiving routing or account number is on the financial institution's negative list.

        A bad example: 
            When can a receiving financial institution use an Accept Without Posting response to an instant credit transfer sent to one of its customer accounts?
               The receiving financial institution's system requires more time to post funds in its core banking system because its communication system is disrupted by a passing asteroid.
               If the recipient is not entitled.
               When the sending or receiving routing or account number is on the financial institution's negative list

    Avoid questions that focus on trivial topics or opinions.
        Write questions that test relevant and important topics.
            WHO, WHAT, WHEN: Facts, useful definitions of terms, critical dates or time frames, monetary limits, significantevents, etc.
            HOW: Proper sequences of actions or behaviors, the next or first step in a process, the most effective technique to use for a given objective/task, etc.
            WHY, SO WHAT: The value of different pieces of information relative to a cause/objective, the rationale for doing something in a particular way, the legal basis for a policy or task, etc.

        A good example:
          According to the Nacha Operating Rules, what must an ODFI’s Origination Agreement include?
             Fees assessed for entries originated
             Restrictions on types of entries originated
             Processing schedules for entries originated

        A bad example:
          Which network rules set has the MOST rules on authorizations?
             FedNow
             Nacha Operating Rules
             RTP

    NACHA Style Guidlines: 
        Word Usage
            Use mitigate in relation to risk rather than “prevent”.​
            Use financial institution instead of specific types of financial institutions (e.g., bank, credit union), unless the type has a bearing on the correct response.​
            Use should instead of “would,” “could,” “might,” or “may.”
        Formatting
            Use italics to emphasize FFIEC source references that are included in the stem. For example:​
                According to the FFIEC IT Examination Handbook: Retail Payment Systems Booklet…​
            Write the word “not” in all capital letters (i.e., NOT).
            Do NOT use underlining or boldface type.
            NEW! Do NOT use the registered trademark or copyright symbols (e.g., RTP®, FedNow®)
        Citations
            Cite the source in the stem if the answer varies depending on which source or perspective one takes.
                Example: “According to Operating Circular 4,…”
            If using a citation to the FFIEC IT Examination Handbook, identify the specific booklet.
                Example: “According to the FFIEC IT Examination Handbook: Retail Payment Systems Booklet…​”
            When referencing the Nacha Operating Rules in an item, always write the full name.
                Do NOT use shortened versions such as ACH Rules or Nacha Rules.​
