Sub-competency 1 — Identify types of value-added and overlay services
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
FPC Glossary, "Overlay Service" definition, lines 619–620:
  "A tailored, value adding payment service or payment-related service
  owned and operated by a third party, which can be deployed on the
  basic infrastructure to deliver value to a specific group of
  subscribers and end users."

FPC Glossary, "Payment Directory Service" definition, lines 646–647:
  "A service that enables a payment directory to facilitate payments
  without requiring sensitive financial account credentials to be shared
  between payee and payor by using some form of an alias associated with
  payment information."

FPC Glossary, "Open Alias Directory" definition, lines 604–607:
  "A directory capability which is available to all approved parties
  under industry accepted governance and access is controlled via
  standards developed and adopted by the industry. Open Alias
  Directories aggregate entries from Private Directories."

FPC Glossary, "Private Directory" definition, lines 706–707:
  "A directory capability which is controlled by a single entity with
  unilateral control over its operation and where access is limited to
  parties who enter into an agreement to secure proprietary access to
  the directory."

AFPP Handbook, lines 8973–8980:
  "Overlay services are specialized offerings that enhance payment
  services by utilizing existing infrastructure to provide value-added
  services (VAS) to users... Overlay services include liquidity
  management, tokenization, biller directory(ies), fraud prevention, and
  customer directory(ies)."

AFPP Handbook, Table 58, lines 9085–9211:
  Comprehensive matrix of overlay service availability by rail:
    Liquidity management tools: FedNow (Yes) | RTP (No) |
                                Same Day ACH (No) | Visa Direct (No) |
                                Mastercard Send (No)
    Exceptions handling: All rails (Yes)
    Alias directories: Same Day ACH (Possible via Zelle) |
                       FedNow (No) | RTP (Possible via Zelle) |
                       Visa Direct (Via Alias Directory Service) |
                       Mastercard Send (Via Consumer Mapping Service)
    Tokenization: RTP (Yes – Secure Token Exchange) |
                  Visa Direct (Token ID service) | Others (No)

Sub-competency 2 — Examples and characteristics of overlay services
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Introduction to the RTP System (October 2020), Section 2.1, page 8:
  "Extensibility – Flexible messaging functionality is included to
  support value-added products. For example, the RTP System provides
  messaging capability enabling a Request for Payment or a Request for
  Information about a Payment."

Introduction to the RTP System, Section 3.1, pages 23–24:
  Remittance Advice (ISO remt.001): "Sent by a Payer's FI to a Payee's
  FI in conjunction with a Credit Transfer to provide remittance
  information to the Payee's FI. It may also be sent in conjunction
  with an RFP as an invoice." Sent when remittance data does not fit
  in the limited fields of the Credit Transfer message itself.

Introduction to the RTP System, Section 3.3, pages 25–27:
  Request for Payment (pain.013): Enables payee-initiated requests;
  supports response options (accept, decline); includes expiry
  management; enables reconciliation between requests and payments.

Introduction to the RTP System, Section 3.4, pages 29–31:
  Request for Information (camt.026): "A multi-use message requesting
  additional information about a Credit Transfer or a Request for
  Payment." Used post-transaction for clarification and to resolve
  discrepancies.

Introduction to the RTP System, Section 2.6, page 20:
  ISO 20022 and value-added services: "The use of ISO 20022 allows RTP
  Participants and TFSPs to develop, source, and introduce new products
  and innovation without requiring customized development which allows
  the U.S. market to move in tandem with global markets to remain
  competitive."

AFPP Handbook, "Request for Payment" subsection, lines 9003–9009:
  "A Request for Payment is a standardized network message that payees
  can leverage to send electronic requests to payers through a faster
  payment network. RfP messages may include reconciliation data for bill
  and invoice presentation, allowing for easier presentation and
  information collection... Billers also benefit from straight-through
  processing (STP) and enhanced reconciliation due to the RfP ISO 20022
  message itself containing the remittance information."

Sub-competency 3 — How overlay services support faster payments rails
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
AFPP Handbook, "Liquidity Management" subsection, lines 9057–9064:
  "Liquidity management [services] help Faster Payment participants
  manage their liquidity. These services are offered by the central
  infrastructure provider and allow participants to make internal
  adjustments or source funds from other FIs to support their liquidity
  needs... These services provide insights into the liquidity positions
  of participant FIs and allow them to monitor their cash flows,
  forecast future liquidity needs, and make proactive transfers to
  prevent service interruptions stemming from liquidity constraints."

AFPP Handbook, "Exceptions Handling" subsection, lines 9067–9072:
  "Exception handling tools allow FIs to identify and manage scenarios
  such as errors, anomalies, and potentially fraudulent activities that
  occur during the payment process. Once exceptions have been identified
  and/or reported, Faster Payment systems can trigger predefined
  workflows to resolve them, including predetermined responses to
  mitigate risks, alerts to relevant stakeholders, and in some cases,
  fund retrieval and/or return."

AFPP Handbook, ISO 20022 rich data, lines 1210–1220:
  "The usage of data-rich messaging standards such as ISO 20022 by
  FedNow and the RTP Network allows for the provision of improved
  payment data... Richer, robust data improves the reconciliation and
  integration process for business end users, making the matching of
  payments to specific invoices easier, and increasing the efficiency
  of e-invoicing and billing."

Q1 — Factual / JSQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: Identify types of overlay services (definition)
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Recall
Rationale: Recognizing the FPC's defining characteristic of an overlay
service — that it is owned and operated by a third party and deployed
on basic infrastructure — is working knowledge for a 2-year faster
payments practitioner.

Stem:
  According to the Faster Payments Council Glossary, which of the
  following best describes an overlay service?

Responses:
  A. A tailored, value-adding payment service owned and operated by a
     third party that is deployed on basic infrastructure to deliver
     value to a specific group of subscribers and end users
  B. A network-level payment routing service operated by a central
     infrastructure provider to support interoperability between faster
     payment rails
  C. A proprietary payment application developed by a financial
     institution to enable customers to initiate payments using a
     mobile device

Answer: A

Source: Glossary of Terms. Faster Payments Council. "Overlay Service"
definition, lines 619–620.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------

Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Recall

payment rail offering a liquidity management overlay service, while RTP
and others do not, requires detailed knowledge of Table 58's rail-by-

Stem:
  According to the AFPP Handbook, on which U.S. faster payment rail is
  a liquidity management overlay service available?

Responses:
  A. Same Day ACH, because the Nacha Operating Rules require
     participants to access liquidity management tools to manage
     settlement exposure between processing windows
  B. The RTP network, because The Clearing House designed its
     prefunding model to integrate with a centralized liquidity
     management system for Funding Participants
  C. FedNow, because the FedNow Service provides participants with
     tools to make internal adjustments and monitor cash flows to
     support their liquidity needs

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 4 – Technology
Considerations for Enabling Faster Payments, Table 58 – Faster Payment
Overlay Services in the US, lines 9085–9211. Also: "Liquidity
Management" subsection, lines 9057–9064.

Q7 — Conditional / JSQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: Examples and characteristics of overlay services
Skill Rating: JSQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: The clear, single-rule condition — that a Remittance Advice
is sent separately when remittance data does not fit in the Credit
Transfer message fields — is a straightforward characteristic of RTP
messaging that a 2-year practitioner handling business payment products
should be able to apply.

Stem:
  Under what condition may a payer's financial institution send a
  Remittance Advice message separately from the Credit Transfer on the
  RTP network?

Responses:
  A. When the remittance information associated with the payment does
     NOT fit into the limited remittance data fields available within
     the Credit Transfer message
  B. When the payee's financial institution requests additional payment
     details through a Request for Information message within 24 hours
     of receiving the Credit Transfer
  C. When the payer's financial institution determines that the payment
     amount exceeds the threshold at which the network requires
     enhanced remittance data for compliance reporting

Answer: A

Source: Introduction to the RTP System (October 2020). The Clearing
House. Section 3.1, "Credit Transfer Event," pages 23–24.

Q8 — Conditional / SQ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-competency: Examples and characteristics of overlay services
Skill Rating: SQ
Cognitive Level: Analysis
Rationale: Distinguishing an open alias directory from a private
directory based on industry-accepted governance, access standards, and
the aggregation of private directory entries requires nuanced knowledge
of directory service architecture beyond 2-year working knowledge.

Stem:
  Under what condition does an alias directory qualify as an open alias
  directory rather than a private directory, according to the Faster
  Payments Council Glossary?

Responses:
  A. When the directory is operated by a central infrastructure
     provider and all registered participants are required to
     contribute alias entries as a condition of network participation
  B. When the directory is available to all approved parties under
     industry-accepted governance, with access controlled via industry-
     developed standards, and aggregates entries from private
     directories
  C. When the directory uses a federated architecture in which each
     participating financial institution maintains its own local alias
     database that synchronizes with a shared central repository

Answer: B

Source: Glossary of Terms. Faster Payments Council. "Open Alias
Directory" definition, lines 604–607. Also: "Private Directory"
definition, lines 706–707.
