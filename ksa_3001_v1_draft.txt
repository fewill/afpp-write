KSA 3003: Customer Onboarding Programs, Policies, and Procedures
Draft Test Questions
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q1 — Factual

Which federal law requires financial institutions to maintain a written
customer identification program?

A. The USA PATRIOT Act
B. The Electronic Funds Transfer Act
C. The Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 3 – Faster Payments Risk
Management, "Customer Identification Program (CIP)" section, lines 8214–8218.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q2 — Factual

How long must a financial institution retain the records used to verify a
customer's identity after the customer's account is closed?

A. Three years
B. Five years
C. Seven years

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 3 – Faster Payments Risk
Management, "Record keeping and retention" subsection, lines 8245–8247.
Regulatory basis: 31 CFR 1010.430.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q3 — Factual

According to the 2026 AFPP Handbook, which party is responsible for sharing
required identity information upon account opening as part of a customer
identification program?

A. The third-party service provider
B. The financial institution
C. The customer

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 3 – Faster Payments Risk
Management, Table 55: "Faster Payments Stakeholder Responsibilities in the
Customer Identification Program," lines 8289–8290.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q4 — Procedural

Which is the first step a financial institution should take when verifying
a customer's identity as part of a customer identification program?

A. Collect the person's name, date of birth, address, and identification number
B. Compare the person's information against the OFAC Specially Designated
   Nationals List
C. File a suspicious activity report if the person's information cannot be
   confirmed

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 3 – Faster Payments Risk
Management, "Verification" subsection, lines 8235–8237.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q5 — Procedural

After a customer account is established, what should a financial institution
do to fulfill its ongoing obligation related to government sanctions lists?

A. Collect updated identification documents from the account holder annually
B. Periodically check account holders against the OFAC Specially Designated
   Nationals List
C. File a suspicious activity report for each account that has not been
   updated within the prior year

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 3 – Faster Payments Risk
Management, "Comparison with government lists" subsection, lines 8255–8257.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q6 — Procedural

When a financial institution relies on another financial institution to
collect customer identification program information, what should the relying
institution verify?

A. That the other financial institution participates in the same faster
   payments network as the relying institution
B. That the other financial institution has filed its annual audit report
   with the applicable network operator
C. That the other financial institution is regulated by a federal regulator
   and has adequate anti-money laundering procedures

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 3 – Faster Payments Risk
Management, Table 55: "Faster Payments Stakeholder Responsibilities in the
Customer Identification Program," lines 8279–8281.

Q7 — Conditional

Under what condition is a financial institution required to file a suspicious
activity report in connection with its customer identification program?

A. The customer identification program fails
B. The customer initiates a faster payment transaction of more than $10,000
C. The customer declines to update personal information after account opening

Answer: A

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 3 – Faster Payments Risk
Management, Table 55: "Faster Payments Stakeholder Responsibilities in the
Customer Identification Program," line 8273.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q8 — Conditional

Why does the irrevocable nature of faster payment transactions make proper
customer onboarding especially important for managing risk?

A. Irrevocable transactions reduce the number of available payment rails,
   requiring more rigorous network registration procedures
B. Irrevocable transactions are more easily abused by fraudsters, increasing
   the legal risks and costs associated with fraud cases
C. Irrevocable transactions require financial institutions to verify customer
   identity more frequently than reversible payment types

Answer: B

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 3 – Faster Payments Risk
Management, "Importance of onboarding programs for managing risk related to
Faster Payments" section, lines 8198–8200.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q9 — Conditional

Under what condition does a non-consumer sender satisfy the legitimate purpose
requirement for sending a Request for Payment message on the RTP network?

A. The message is accompanied by a signed origination agreement between the
   sending and receiving financial institutions
B. The message is initiated to a receiver who is known to the sender and who
   is reasonably expected to receive the request
C. The message is sent for a current sale or transaction, or the amount is
   due, owed, or otherwise agreed to be paid to the sender

Answer: C

Source: AFPP Handbook (2026). Nacha. Chapter 3 – Faster Payments Risk
Management, "Additional requirements for Faster Payment customers that use
Request-for-Payment functionality," lines 8300–8301.
